## üëë Slave Dynasty (1206-1290)

### Qutbuddin Aibak (1206-1210)

:::details **Foundation**
- **First**: Sultan of Delhi
- **Slave**: Of Muhammad Ghori
- **1206**: Sultanate established formally
- **Capital**: Lahore initially chosen
:::

:::details **Achievements**
- **Qutub**: Minar construction started
- **Quwwat-ul-Islam**: Mosque built Delhi
- **Liberal**: Ruler generous donations
- **Lakh**: Baksh title given
:::

:::details **Death**
- **1210**: Polo accident died
- **Lahore**: Buried there permanently
:::

### Iltutmish (1211-1236)

:::details **Consolidation**
- **Real**: Founder sultanate actual
- **Forty**: Nobles Turkan-i-Chahalgani
- **Capital**: Delhi from Lahore
- **Mongols**: Repelled invasions successfully
:::

:::details **Administrative Reforms**
- **Iqta**: System organized properly
- **Coinage**: Silver Tanka introduced
- **Chahalgani**: Forty nobles group
- **Centralization**: Attempted administrative reforms
:::

:::details **Achievements**
- **Qutub**: Minar completed structure
- **Caliph**: Recognition received 1229
- **Bengal**: Conquered Bihar regions
- **Razia**: Nominated daughter successor
:::

### Razia Sultan (1236-1240)

:::details **First Woman Ruler**
- **Only**: Woman sultan Delhi
- **Capable**: Ruler efficient administrator
- **Father**: Iltutmish nominated her
- **Opposition**: Nobles resisted strongly
:::

:::details **Reign**
- **Male**: Attire wore publicly
- **Darbar**: Held without purdah
- **Reforms**: Administrative changes attempted
- **Abyssinian**: Slave Yakut favored
:::

:::details **Downfall**
- **Nobles**: Conspired against her
- **1240**: Defeated, killed brutally
- **Brief**: Reign four years
:::

### Balban (1266-1287)

:::details **Theory of Kingship**
- **Divine**: Right theory propounded
- **Sijda**: Prostration introduced court
- **Paibos**: Kissing feet custom
- **Strong**: Monarchy established firmly
:::

:::details **Administrative Reforms**
- **Spy**: System diwan-i-arz
- **Blood**: Iron policy followed
- **Mongols**: Repelled repeatedly successfully
- **Forty**: Nobles broke power
:::

:::details **Military Measures**
- **Army**: Strengthened considerably increased
- **Forts**: Built northwestern frontier
- **Mongols**: Defended invasions multiple
- **Law**: Order restored completely
:::

:::details **Mongol Invasions Repelled**
- **Multiple**: Invasions defeated successfully
- **1299**: Defeated near Delhi
- **1303**: Siege Delhi lifted
- **Strong**: Defense prevented invasions
:::

:::details **Administrative Reforms**

:::details **Religious Policy**

## üëë Tughlaq Dynasty (1320-1414)

### Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (1320-1325)

:::details **Foundation**
- **1320**: Established Tughlaq dynasty
- **Strong**: Ruler capable administrator
- **Revenue**: Reforms moderate approach
- **Canals**: Irrigation encouraged development
:::

:::details **Death**
- **1325**: Wooden pavilion collapsed
- **Suspicious**: Death conspiracy suspected
- **Son**: Muhammad succeeded him
:::

### Muhammad bin Tughlaq (1325-1351)

:::details **Five Failed Experiments**
- **Token**: Currency copper introduced
- **Capital**: Transfer Daulatabad attempted
- **Taxation**: Doab heavy burden
- **Khurasan**: Expedition abandoned plan
- **Qarachi**: Expedition Himalayas failed
:::

:::details **Token Currency**
- **Copper**: Coins introduced suddenly
- **Forgery**: Rampant counterfeiting occurred
- **Failure**: Complete economic disaster
- **Withdrew**: Exchanged for silver
:::

:::details **Capital Transfer**
- **Daulatabad**: Deccan shifted capital
- **Forced**: Migration people Delhi
- **Failure**: Impractical decision proved
- **Returned**: Delhi after failure
:::

:::details **Doab Taxation**
- **Heavy**: Tax burden imposed
- **Famine**: Resulted in region
- **Rebellion**: People revolted against
- **Failure**: Revenue declined drastically
:::

:::details **Traveler Account**
- **Ibn**: Battuta visited court
- **Qazi**: Appointed as judge
- **Genius**: Mad called him
- **Misfortune**: With fortunes description
:::
