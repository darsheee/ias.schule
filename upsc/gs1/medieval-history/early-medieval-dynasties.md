## üëë Pala Dynasty (750-1174 CE)

### Major Rulers

:::details **Gopala I (750-770)**
- **Founder**: Pala dynasty
- **Democratic**: Elected by nobles
- **Bengal**: Region controlled
- **Stability**: Brought order
:::

:::details **Dharmapala (770-810)**
- **Son**: Gopala succeeded
- **Kannauj**: Captured briefly
- **Triangular**: Warfare participant
- **North**: Expansion attempted
- **Patron**: Buddhism supported
:::

:::details **Devapala (810-850)**
- **Grandson**: Dharmapala's grandson
- **Pinnacle**: Pala power reached
- **Assam**: Conquered region
- **Orissa**: Controlled territories
- **Patron**: Mahayana Buddhism
:::

:::details **Mahipala I (988-1038)**
- **Revival**: Pala power restored
- **Bengal**: Reunited territories
- **Last**: Great Pala ruler
- **Decline**: Started after him
:::

### Administration & Society

:::details **Administration**
- **Feudal**: System developed
- **Land**: Grants to nobles
- **Military**: Based on feudal lords
- **Revenue**: Land collection main
:::

:::details **Religion**
- **Buddhism**: Main religion patronized
- **Mahayana**: Buddhism dominant
- **Tantric**: Buddhism flourished
- **Nalanda**: University patronized
- **Vikramashila**: University founded
:::

:::details **Art & Architecture**
- **Pala**: School of art developed
- **Sculpture**: Buddhist statues
- **Manuscripts**: Buddhist texts illustrated
- **Stupas**: Built extensively
:::

## üëë Pratihara Dynasty (736-1036 CE)

### Major Rulers

:::details **Nagabhata I (736-760)**
- **Founder**: Pratihara dynasty
- **Gurjar**: Region controlled
- **Arabs**: Repelled invasions
- **Western**: India protected
:::

:::details **Vatsaraja (775-800)**
- **Expansion**: Northward conquests
- **Kannauj**: Captured briefly
- **Rashtrakutas**: Defeated initially
- **Triangular**: Warfare active
:::

:::details **Nagabhata II (800-833)**
- **Kannauj**: Captured successfully
- **Control**: Doab region
- **Rashtrakutas**: Defeated again
- **Powerful**: Strong ruler
:::

:::details **Mihira Bhoja (836-885)**
- **Greatest**: Pratihara ruler
- **Kannauj**: Firm control established
- **Conquests**: Extensive territories
- **Patron**: Literature, arts
- **Aihole**: Inscription praised him
:::

:::details **Mahipala I (912-944)**
- **Weak**: Ruler ineffective
- **Decline**: Started period
- **Rashtrakutas**: Lost to
- **Territory**: Reduced gradually
:::

### Administration & Society

:::details **Administration**
- **Feudal**: System strong
- **Military**: Based on feudal lords
- **Revenue**: Land collection efficient
- **Centralized**: Attempted control
:::

:::details **Religion**
- **Hinduism**: Main religion
- **Shaivism**: Popular sect
- **Vaishnavism**: Also followed
- **Temples**: Built extensively
:::

:::details **Art & Architecture**
- **Temple**: Building activity
- **Khajuraho**: Temples built
- **Gwalior**: Fort constructed
- **Sculpture**: Hindu gods depicted
:::

## üëë Rashtrakuta Dynasty (753-973 CE)

### Major Rulers

:::details **Dantidurga (753-756)**
- **Founder**: Rashtrakuta dynasty
- **Chalukyas**: Overthrew Badami
- **Deccan**: Controlled region
- **Expansion**: Started conquests
:::

:::details **Krishna I (756-774)**
- **Son**: Dantidurga succeeded
- **Kailash**: Temple Ellora built
- **Architecture**: Magnificent structures
- **Expansion**: Continued conquests
:::

:::details **Govinda II (774-780)**
- **Son**: Krishna I succeeded
- **Religious**: Inclined ruler
- **Abdicated**: Voluntarily throne
:::

:::details **Dhruva (780-793)**
- **Uncle**: Govinda II replaced
- **Powerful**: Strong military
- **North**: Campaigns successful
- **Kannauj**: Captured briefly
- **Control**: Extensive territories
:::

:::details **Govinda III (793-814)**
- **Son**: Dhruva succeeded
- **Greatest**: Rashtrakuta ruler
- **Conquests**: Maximum expansion
- **North**: India controlled briefly
- **South**: India dominated
:::

:::details **Amoghavarsha I (814-878)**
- **Son**: Govinda III succeeded
- **Long**: Reign 64 years
- **Peace**: Maintained stability
- **Patron**: Literature, arts
- **Kavirajamarga**: First Kannada work
:::

### Administration & Society

:::details **Administration**
- **Centralized**: Strong system
- **Military**: Well-organized army
- **Revenue**: Efficient collection
- **Provincial**: Governors appointed
:::

:::details **Religion**
- **Hinduism**: Main religion
- **Jainism**: Also patronized
- **Buddhism**: Some support
- **Temples**: Built extensively
:::

:::details **Art & Architecture**
- **Ellora**: Caves magnificent
- **Kailash**: Temple masterpiece
- **Shiva**: Temple monolithic
- **Sculpture**: Hindu, Jain themes
:::
