# History of British India
## Complete Chronological Timeline (1599-1947)

A comprehensive chronology of British India from the founding of the East India Company to Independence and Partition. This timeline traces the transformation from commercial enterprise to territorial empire, covering major battles, administrative developments, social reforms, and the rise of Indian nationalism.

## üìú Major Legislative Acts

### British Parliament Acts

**1773 - Regulating Act**
- Created Governor-General position
- Established Supreme Court at Calcutta
- Company under Parliamentary supervision

**1784 - Pitt's India Act**
- Created Board of Control
- Dual control: Company and Crown

**1813 - Charter Act**
- Ended Company's trade monopoly (except China tea)
- Christian missionaries allowed
- ¬£100,000 for education

**1833 - Charter Act**
- Company became purely administrative
- Governor-General of India (not just Bengal)
- Slavery abolished

**1853 - Charter Act**
- Open competition for Civil Service
- Legislative and executive functions separated

**1858 - Government of India Act**
- Company rule ended
- Crown assumed direct control
- Secretary of State for India created

**1861 - Indian Councils Act**
- Legislative councils expanded
- Portfolio system introduced

**1892 - Indian Councils Act**
- Increased non-official members
- Limited discussion powers

**1909 - Indian Councils Act (Morley-Minto Reforms)**
- Separate electorates for Muslims
- Expanded legislative councils

**1919 - Government of India Act (Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms)**
- Dyarchy in provinces
- Bicameral central legislature

**1935 - Government of India Act**
- Provincial autonomy
- Federal structure (not fully implemented)
- Separate electorates continued

**1947 - Indian Independence Act**
- Two dominions: India and Pakistan
- Transfer of power
