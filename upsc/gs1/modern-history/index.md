# Modern Indian History
## From Fall of Mughals to Independence (1707-1947)

Modern Indian History covers the period from the decline of the Mughal Empire to India's independence, including British colonial rule and the freedom struggle.

### [1. Fall of Mughals](/upsc/gs1/modern-history/fall-of-mughals)

- **Regional Powers**: Emerged rapidly
- **Maratha Expansion**: Challenged Mughals

- **Portuguese**: Arrived 1498 Vasco
- **Dutch**: Trading posts established
- **French**: Pondicherry base created
- **British**: East India Company
- **Competition**: For trade dominance

- **Battle of Plassey**: 1757 victory

- **Expansion**: Territorial gains rapid
- **Reforms**: Administrative changes made

- **Governor-Generals**: Key figures listed
- **Acts**: Regulating, Charter Acts

- **Civil Services**: Recruitment system

### [5. British Political Policies](/upsc/gs1/modern-history/political-policies)
- **Subsidiary Alliance**: Wellesley introduced
- **Doctrine of Lapse**: Dalhousie policy
- **Annexations**: Territories absorbed systematically
- **Princely States**: Relations managed
- **Divide & Rule**: Strategy employed

### [6. British Expansion](/upsc/gs1/modern-history/british-expansion)
- **Anglo-Mysore Wars**: Four wars

- **Conquest Completed**: By 1857
- **Resistance**: Indian rulers fought

- **Drain of Wealth**: Systematic exploitation

- **Land Revenue**: Systems introduced
- **Commercialization**: Agriculture changed fundamentally
- **Railways**: Infrastructure for exploitation

- **Punjab**: Ranjit Singh united
