## üìö Detailed Content Clusters for UPSC Preparation

:::details **THE REVOLT OF 1857**
- **Causes**:
  - Political: Doctrine of Lapse (Dalhousie's policy to annex states without natural heirs), annexation of Awadh under pretext of misgovernance
  - Economic: High land revenue, destruction of traditional industries, exploitation of resources
  - Military: Discrimination against Indian soldiers in pay and promotion, introduction of Enfield rifle cartridges
  - Religious: Fear of Christianization, interference in religious customs

- **Nature and Perspectives**:
  - Military revolt perspective: Primarily a mutiny of sepoys that spread to civilians
  - First War of Independence perspective: V.D. Savarkar called it India's "First War of Independence"
  - Regional variations: Strong in North India, weak or absent in Southern regions
  - Historian R.C. Mazumdar view: Had localized, constrained, poorly organized character rather than all-India character

- **Significance**:
  - End of East India Company rule and beginning of direct Crown rule
  - Policy changes: Queen's Proclamation (1858) promised non-interference in religion, respect for princely states
  - Military reorganization: Reduced Indian soldiers in army, increased British troops
  - Administrative changes: Secretary of State replaced Board of Control
:::

:::details **SOCIO-RELIGIOUS REFORM MOVEMENTS**
- **Hindu Reform Movements**:
  - Raja Ram Mohan Roy & Brahmo Samaj (1828):
    - Opposed Sati (abolished in 1829), polygamy, child marriage
    - Promoted monotheism, modern education (helped establish Hindu College)
    - Works: Tuhfat-ul-Muwahhidin, Precepts of Jesus, translations of Vedas/Upanishads

  - Dayanand Saraswati & Arya Samaj (1875):
    - Revivalist movement promoting Vedic traditions
    - Established D.A.V. Colleges (1886)
    - Shuddhi (purification) movement to bring converts back to Hinduism
    - Works: Satyarth Prakash

  - Young Bengal Movement (Henry Vivian Derozio):
    - Radical intellectual movement from Hindu College, Kolkata
    - Promoted rational thinking and scientific approach
    - Considered first nationalist poet of modern India

- **Muslim Reform Movements**:
  - Deoband Movement (Revivalist):
    - Focused on moral/religious renewal of Muslim community
    - Opposed Western education and British rule
    - Encouraged spirit of Jihad against British

  - Aligarh Movement (Reformist - Sir Syed Ahmed Khan):
    - Promoted modern education and scientific learning
    - Established Mohammedan Anglo-Oriental College (later AMU)
    - Initially encouraged loyalty to British government

- **Other Reform Movements**:
  - Sikh: Singh Sabha (1870s), Nirankari Movement (1855), Akali Movement for Gurdwara reforms
  - Parsi: Rahnumae Mazdayasnan Sabha (1851) led by Dadabhai Naoroji, fought against orthodox practices
  - Women-focused: Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar's campaign for widow remarriage (1856 Act)
:::

:::details **BRITISH COLONIAL POLICIES & ADMINISTRATION**
- **Expansion Policies**:
  - Doctrine of Lapse (1848-1856): Annexed states like Satara, Jhansi, Nagpur, Sambalpur
  - Doctrine of Misgovernance: Annexation of Awadh (1856) under pretext of maladministration
  - Subsidiary Alliance System: States surrendered foreign policy, maintained British troops

- **Administrative Evolution**:
  - Pitt's India Act (1784): Established Board of Control in London
  - Charter Acts (1793, 1813, 1833, 1853): Gradually reduced Company's commercial privileges
  - Crown Rule (1858): After 1857 revolt, power transferred from Company to British Crown
  - Features of early administration: Government by councils, boards (Trade, Revenue, Military), record-keeping

- **British Success Factors**:
  - Superior military technology and organization
  - Divided Indian rulers and effective diplomacy
  - Financial strength and control of seas
  - Efficient administrative structure
  - Policy of divide and rule
:::

:::details **INDIAN NATIONAL CONGRESS & EARLY NATIONALISM**
- **Formation and Early Years**:
  - Founded in 1885 by A.O. Hume, first president W.C. Banerjee
  - Initial moderate phase (1885-1905): Constitutional methods, petitions, prayers
  - Moderates' approach: Used legislative councils, held discussions, formed associations
  - Leaders: Dadabhai Naoroji, Gopal Krishna Gokhale, Pherozeshah Mehta

- **Partition of Bengal & Swadeshi Movement (1905)**:
  - Curzon's partition on communal basis sparked nationwide protests
  - Boycott of British goods, promotion of Swadeshi enterprises
  - Emergence of militant nationalism and revolutionary terrorism

- **Surat Split (1907)**:
  - Division between Moderates and Extremists over methods
  - Extremists led by Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Bipin Chandra Pal advocated more aggressive approach
  - Reunified at Lucknow Session (1916)

- **Morley-Minto Reforms (1909)**:
  - Increased Indian representation in legislative councils
  - Introduced separate electorates for Muslims (divisive policy)
  - Disappointed nationalists seeking self-government
:::

:::details **GANDHIAN ERA & MASS MOVEMENTS (1918-1939)**
- **Gandhi's Early Experiments**:
  - South African experience (1893-1914): Developed Satyagraha technique
  - Champaran (1917), Kheda (1918), Ahmedabad Mill Strike (1918) - first Satyagrahas in India
  - Key ideologies: Satyagraha (truth-force), Ahimsa (non-violence), Sarvodaya (welfare of all)

- **Major Movements**:
  - Khilafat and Non-Cooperation Movement (1919-22):
    - Hindu-Muslim unity against Rowlatt Act and Khilafat issue
    - Chauri Chaura incident led to withdrawal of movement

  - Civil Disobedience Movement (1930-34):
    - Dandi March (March 12-April 6, 1930) against salt tax
    - Gandhi-Irwin Pact (1931): Congress participation in Round Table Conference
    - Poona Pact (1932): Ambedkar-Gandhi agreement on separate electorates for depressed classes

  - Government of India Act 1935:
    - Provincial autonomy, federal structure (never implemented fully)
    - Congress ministries formed in 8 provinces after 1937 elections

- **Ideological Development**:
  - Purna Swaraj declaration (1929): Complete independence as goal
  - Constructive Programme: Khadi, Harijan welfare, Hindu-Muslim unity, prohibition
  - Concept of trusteeship for economic equality
:::

:::details **REVOLUTIONARY MOVEMENTS & PERSONALITIES**
- **Revolutionary Organizations**:
  - Abhinav Bharat Society (1904): Founded by Savarkar brothers
  - Anushilan Samiti & Jugantar Group: Bengal-based revolutionary organizations
  - Ghadar Movement (1913): Indian diaspora-led revolutionary movement
  - Hindustan Republican Association (1924): Later became Hindustan Socialist Republican Association
  - Revolutionary methods: Individual heroic actions, armed resistance, propaganda

- **Key Personalities**:
  - Bal Gangadhar Tilak: "Lokmanya," extremist leader, founder of Home Rule League
  - Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, Sukhdev: HSRA revolutionaries, executed in 1931
  - Chandrashekhar Azad: Martyred in 1931 after police encounter
  - Vinayak Damodar Savarkar: Formulated concept of Hindutva, revolutionary turned politician
  - Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel: "Iron Man of India," led Bardoli Satyagraha
  - Sarojini Naidu: "Nightingale of India," participated in Civil Disobedience Movement

- **Komagata Maru Incident (1914)**:
  - Ship carrying Indian immigrants denied entry to Canada
  - When returned to India, passengers faced police firing at Budge Budge
  - Sparked anti-British sentiment in Punjab
:::

:::details **POLITICAL DEVELOPMENTS & CONSTITUTIONAL REFORMS**
- **Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919)**:
  - Dyarchy system in provinces: Divided subjects into reserved and transferred
  - Increased Indian representation but limited powers
  - Led to formation of Swaraj Party (1923) by C.R. Das and Motilal Nehru

- **Simon Commission (1928)**:
  - All-British commission to review constitutional reforms
  - Boycotted by all parties with slogan "Simon Go Back"
  - Led to Nehru Report (1928) drafted by committee chaired by Motilal Nehru

- **Round Table Conferences (1930-32)**:
  - Three conferences in London to discuss constitutional reforms
  - Gandhi represented Congress in second conference (1931)
  - Communal Award (1932): Separate electorates for minorities, opposed by Gandhi

- **Lucknow Pact (1916)**:
  - Congress-Muslim League agreement on constitutional reforms
  - Significance: Hindu-Muslim unity, joint constitutional demands
  - Criticism: Recognized communal representation and privileges
  - Long-term impact: Legitimized Muslim League's communal politics

- **Home Rule Movement (1916)**:
  - Annie Besant and Bal Gangadhar Tilak established Home Rule Leagues
  - Pushed government toward Mont-Ford reforms
  - Established organizational links between towns and countryside
:::

:::details **ROLE OF DIFFERENT SECTIONS IN FREEDOM STRUGGLE**
- **Women's Participation**:
  - Early reform movements for women's rights (education, suffrage)
  - Active role in Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience movements
  - Leaders: Sarojini Naidu, Kamaladevi Chattopadhyay, Aruna Asaf Ali
  - Mass participation in protests, picketing of liquor shops

- **Business Communities**:
  - Early support through funding and organization
  - Organizations: FICCI (1927), Indian Merchants' Chamber
  - Leaders: G.D. Birla, Jamnalal Bajaj provided financial support to Congress
  - Shifted between cooperation and confrontation based on economic interests

- **Working Class & Peasants**:
  - Trade union movements from 1920s (AITUC formation 1920)
  - Communist influence in labor and peasant movements
  - Regional peasant movements: Tebhaga (Bengal), Punnapra-Vayalar (Travancore)

- **Princely States**:
  - Complex relationship with nationalist movement
  - People's movements against autocratic rulers in states like Travancore, Hyderabad
  - Integration challenges after independence

- **Foreign Support**:
  - International dimension through organizations like League Against Imperialism
  - Support from anti-colonial movements worldwide
  - Influence of socialist and anti-fascist movements
:::

:::details **BRITISH FOREIGN POLICY IN INDIA**
- **Strategic Considerations**:
  - Securing boundaries of conquered territories
  - Protection against Russian expansion (Great Game)
  - Control of key strategic locations (Suez, Malacca, Cape)

- **Relations with Neighboring States**:
  - Sikkim: Declared British protectorate (1861), recognized by China (1890)
  - Bhutan: Treaty of Punakha (1910) gave control over foreign relations to British
  - Afghanistan: Complex relations, two Anglo-Afghan wars (1839-42, 1878-80)
  - Nepal: Treaty of Sugauli (1816) after Anglo-Nepalese War

- **Impact of World Wars**:
  - World War I: India contributed troops and resources, expected political concessions
  - Rowlatt Act (1919) and Jallianwala Bagh massacre aftermath
  - World War II: Cripps Mission (1942), Quit India Movement, INA trials
  - Changed international context accelerated decolonization process

- **Transfer of Power**:
  - Cabinet Mission Plan (1946): Attempt to preserve united India
  - Mountbatten Plan and Indian Independence Act (1947)
  - Partition and challenges of independence
:::

:::details **LIFE AND WORKS OF KEY PERSONALITIES**
- **Mahatma Gandhi**:
  - Early life: Born October 2, 1869 in Porbandar, studied law in England, practiced in South Africa (1893-1914)
  - South Africa experiments: Developed Satyagraha technique, fought against racial discrimination, established Phoenix Settlement and Tolstoy Farm
  - Key works: Hind Swaraj (1909), Autobiography: The Story of My Experiments with Truth
  - Return to India: January 9, 1915, established Sabarmati Ashram in 1916
  - Ideologies: Satyagraha (truth-force), Ahimsa (non-violence), Sarvodaya (welfare of all), Trusteeship

- **Bal Gangadhar Tilak**:
  - "Lokmanya" Tilak: Called "Father of Indian Unrest" by British
  - Founded home rule league in 1916, established newspapers Kesari and Mahratta
  - Famous quote: "Swaraj is my birthright and I shall have it"
  - Books: Gita Rahasya, Arctic Home in the Vedas

- **Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel**:
  - "Iron Man of India": Led Bardoli Satyagraha (1928)
  - Instrumental in integration of 562 princely states after independence
  - First Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of independent India
  - Key role in Congress organization and party discipline

- **Dr. B.R. Ambedkar**:
  - Drafting committee chairman for Indian Constitution
  - Fought against caste discrimination, organized Mahad Satyagraha (1927)
  - Founded Scheduled Castes Federation (1942)
  - Works: Annihilation of Caste, The Problem of the Rupee
:::

:::details **INTERNATIONAL DIMENSIONS & FOREIGN SUPPORT**
- **International Context**:
  - League Against Imperialism: Formed in 1927 with Indian representatives
  - Global anti-colonial movements inspired Indian freedom fighters
  - Impact of Russian Revolution (1917) on Indian communist movement
  - Support from international figures like Albert Einstein, Pearl S. Buck

- **Role of Indian Diaspora**:
  - Ghadar Movement (1913): Based in USA and Canada, led by Lala Har Dayal, Sohan Singh Bhakna
  - Komagata Maru Incident (1914): Japanese ship carrying 376 Indian immigrants denied entry to Canada
  - Indian Independence League (Japan/SE Asia): Led by Rash Behari Bose, later Subhash Chandra Bose
  - Role of V.D. Savarkar, Madame Cama, Shyamji Krishna Varma in European exile

- **World War Impact**:
  - WWI: India contributed 1.3 million soldiers, expected political concessions post-war
  - WWII: Strategic importance of India increased, Cripps Mission (1942), Quit India Movement
  - International pressure on Britain to decolonize after WWII
  - American support for Indian independence under Roosevelt administration
:::

:::details **COMMUNAL QUESTION & MUSLIM POLITICS**
- **Evolution of Muslim Politics**:
  - Formation of Muslim League (1906): Initially loyalist, later became vehicle for separatist demands
  - Lucknow Pact (1916): Congress-League agreement on separate electorates
  - Khilafat Movement (1919-22): Hindu-Muslim unity against abolition of Ottoman Caliphate
  - Nehru Report (1928) vs Jinnah's Fourteen Points (1929)

- **Key Events**:
  - Communal Award (1932): Separate electorates for depressed classes, opposed by Gandhi
  - Poona Pact (1932): Gandhi-Ambedkar agreement on reserved seats instead of separate electorates
  - Provincial elections (1937): Congress victory in 8 provinces, League's poor performance
  - Lahore Resolution (1940): First formal demand for Pakistan
  - Cripps Mission (1942): Failed due to disagreements over Pakistan issue
  - Cabinet Mission Plan (1946): Last attempt to preserve united India

- **Ideological Dimensions**:
  - Two-nation theory: Developed by Iqbal, propagated by Jinnah
  - Congress secularism: Composite nationalism concept
  - Role of Hindu Mahasabha and RSS in communal polarization
  - Gandhi-Jinnah talks (1944) failure
:::

:::details **SOCIALIST & COMMUNIST INFLUENCE**
- **Emergence of Left Politics**:
  - Foundation of Communist Party of India (1925)
  - Meerut Conspiracy Case (1929): Suppression of communist leaders
  - Congress Socialist Party (1934): Formed within Congress by Nehru, JP Narayan, Acharya Narendra Dev
  - Kisan Sabha movements: All India Kisan Sabha founded in 1936

- **Key Ideologies and Movements**:
  - Marxism-Leninism influence on Indian politics
  - Trade union movements: All India Trade Union Congress (AITUC) formation (1920)
  - Peasant movements: Tebhaga movement (Bengal), Punnapra-Vayalar uprising (Travancore)
  - Revolutionary socialism: Bhagat Singh's HSRA, Chittagong Armory Raid (1930)

- **Impact on Freedom Struggle**:
  - Ideological challenge to Gandhian non-violence
  - Greater focus on economic exploitation and class struggle
  - Introduction of workers and peasants as organized political forces
  - Influence on post-independence economic planning
:::

:::details **PRINCELY STATES & INTEGRATION CHALLENGES**
- **Relationship with National Movement**:
  - Complex relationship between Princely States and nationalist movement
  - States People's Conference formed in 1927 to coordinate movements
  - Regional movements: Praja Mandals in various states (Rajasthan, Central India)
  - Travancore: Temple entry movement, formation of Travancore State Congress
  - Hyderabad: Osmania University students' movement, Telangana armed struggle

- **Integration Process**:
  - Sardar Patel's role as States Minister after 1947
  - Instrument of Accession signed by rulers of 562 princely states
  - Challenges: Junagadh, Hyderabad, Kashmir accession issues
  - Role of V.P. Menon as constitutional advisor to Patel
  - Military operations: Operation Polo (Hyderabad, 1948), Operation Vijay (Goa, 1961)

- **Legacy of Princely States**:
  - Administrative integration challenges
  - Privy purses controversy and eventual abolition (1971)
  - Cultural heritage preservation
  - Contemporary issues of regional identity
:::

:::details **FINAL PHASE & TRANSFER OF POWER (1940-1947)**
- **Key Events**:
  - August Offer (1940): Dominion status promise after war
  - Individual Satyagraha (1940): Limited protest against war involvement
  - Cripps Mission (1942): Failed constitutional proposals
  - Quit India Movement (August 1942): "Do or Die" call, mass arrests
  - INA Trials (1945-46): Red Fort trials sparked nationwide protests
  - Naval Mutiny (1946): 20,000 sailors across 78 ships participated
  - Cabinet Mission Plan (1946): Last attempt to preserve united India
  - Mountbatten Plan (June 1947): Partition and independence

- **Mountbatten's Role**:
  - Accelerated transfer of power timeline
  - Boundary Commission chaired by Radcliffe
  - Finalized June 3 Plan or Mountbatten Plan
  - Oversaw partition process and independence ceremonies

- **Legacy**:
  - Partition violence: Estimated 1 million deaths, 14 million displaced
  - Constitutional developments leading to Republic (1950)
  - Unfinished agenda: Kashmir, refugee rehabilitation, princely state integration
  - Nehru's "Tryst with Destiny" speech (August 14-15, 1947)
:::
