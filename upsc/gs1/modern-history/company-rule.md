## üèõÔ∏è Governor-Generals of Bengal

:::details **Robert Clive (1757-1760, 1765-1767)**
- **Battle of Plassey**: 1757 won
- **Dual Government**: Introduced system
- **Treaty of Allahabad**: 1765 signed
- **Reforms**: Administrative changes
- **Society of Trade**: Abolished monopoly
:::

:::details **Warren Hastings (1772-1785)**
- **First Governor-General**: Bengal 1772
- **Dual Government**: Abolished 1772
- **Regulating Act**: 1773 implemented
- **Supreme Court**: Calcutta established
- **Rohilla War**: 1774 fought
- **First Anglo-Maratha War**: 1775-1782
- **Second Anglo-Mysore War**: 1780-1784
- **Impeachment**: England faced trial
:::

:::details **Lord Cornwallis (1786-1793)**
- **Permanent Settlement**: 1793 Bengal
- **Police System**: Reorganized completely
- **Judiciary**: Separated from executive
- **Civil Services**: Covenanted system
- **Third Anglo-Mysore War**: 1790-1792
- **Code of Regulations**: 1793 introduced
:::

:::details **Lord Wellesley (1798-1805)**
- **Subsidiary Alliance**: System introduced
- **Fourth Anglo-Mysore War**: 1799 Tipu
- **Second Anglo-Maratha War**: 1803-1805
- **Expansion**: Territorial gains massive
- **Fort William College**: 1800 established
:::

:::details **Lord Hastings (1813-1823)**
- **Third Anglo-Maratha War**: 1817-1819
- **Pindari War**: Suppressed completely
- **Anglo-Nepal War**: 1814-1816
- **Maratha Power**: Ended finally
- **Ryotwari System**: Introduced Madras
:::

:::details **Lord William Bentinck (1828-1835)**
- **Social Reforms**: Most progressive
- **Sati Abolished**: 1829 Act
- **Thuggee Suppressed**: Criminal tribes
- **English Education**: Macaulay's Minute
- **Charter Act**: 1833 implemented
- **Mysore Annexed**: 1831 temporarily
:::

:::details **Lord Dalhousie (1848-1856)**
- **Doctrine of Lapse**: Annexation policy
- **Second Anglo-Sikh War**: 1848-1849
- **Punjab Annexed**: 1849 completely
- **Railways**: First 1853 introduced
- **Telegraph**: Introduced communication
- **Postal System**: Modernized completely
- **Widow Remarriage**: 1856 legalized
- **Wood's Despatch**: 1854 education
:::

## üìã Important Acts

:::details **Regulating Act 1773**
- **First Step**: Parliamentary control
- **Governor-General**: Bengal created
- **Supreme Court**: Calcutta established
- **Council**: Four members
- **Warren Hastings**: First Governor-General
:::

:::details **Pitt's India Act 1784**
- **Dual Government**: Britain established
- **Board of Control**: Created new
- **Court of Directors**: Commercial matters
- **Board**: Political affairs
- **Governor-General**: Strengthened powers
:::

:::details **Charter Act 1813**
- **Trade Monopoly**: Ended except tea
- **Missionary Activities**: Allowed officially
- **Education**: 1 lakh allocation
- **Company Rule**: Extended 20 years
:::

:::details **Charter Act 1833**
- **Trade Monopoly**: Completely ended
- **Governor-General**: All India
- **First Law Member**: Macaulay appointed
- **Slavery**: Abolished India
- **Civil Services**: Open competition
:::

:::details **Charter Act 1853**
- **Separate Legislature**: First time
- **Civil Services**: Competitive examination
- **Company Rule**: Extended indefinitely
- **Governor-General**: Executive council
:::
