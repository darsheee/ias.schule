# Imperial India
## Queen Victoria as Empress & Age of Curzon (1877-1905)

This period marks India's imperial zenith, opening with Queen Victoria's proclamation as Empress of India and closing with Lord Curzon's controversial viceroyalty. Key developments include the Second Afghan War creating the legendary "Kabul to Kandahar March," the Ilbert Bill controversy over racial equality in jurisprudence, the formation of the Indian National Congress (1885), military reforms, famine relief policies, and Curzon's administrative overreach leading to his resignation.

Major Cavagnari met Afghan chiefs at Jalalabad
:::

:::details **8 Jan 1879 - Khost & Kandahar**
Roberts occupied Khost district; Gen Sir Donald Stewart occupied Kandahar
:::

:::details **21 Feb 1879 - New Amir**
Sher Ali died at Mazar-i-Sharif; Yakub Khan became Amir

### Treaty & Renewed Conflict (1879)

:::details **26 May 1879 - Treaty of Gandamak**
Cavagnari-Yakub Khan agreement:
- British controlled: Kurram Valley, Sibi, Pishin, Khyber Pass, Michni Pass
- British controlled Afghan foreign policy
- British representative in Kabul
- Annual subsidy: 6 lakhs rupees
- **Ratified**: 30 May 1879
:::

:::details **24 July 1879 - Cavagnari Arrives Kabul**
Cavagnari and escort arrived at Kabul
:::

:::details **30 Sept 1879 - KABUL MISSION MASSACRE**
Afghan soldiers and mob killed entire British Mission including Cavagnari
:::

### Roberts' Kabul Campaign (1879-1880)

:::details **1 Oct 1879 - Kabul Field Force**
Roberts led Kabul Field Force (6,600 men, 18 guns) across Shutagardan Pass
:::

:::details **6 Oct 1879 - Battle of Charasia**
Roberts defeated 8,000-10,000 Afghans
- British losses: 18 killed, 70 wounded
- Afghan losses: 500 killed
:::

:::details **9 Oct 1879 - Kabul Taken**
Roberts took control of Kabul
:::

:::details **16 Oct 1879 - Bala Hissar Explosion**
Gunpowder explosion at Bala Hissar fort (mysterious circumstances)
:::

:::details **Oct-Nov 1879 - War Trials**
163 men tried, 89 executed in Kabul
:::

:::details **28 Oct 1879 - Yakub Khan Resigned**
Yakub Khan resigned as Amir
:::

:::details **8-14 Dec 1879 - Near Kabul Actions**
Roberts fought unsuccessful actions near Kabul: 81 killed, 213 wounded
:::

:::details **22/23 Dec 1879 - Battle of Sherpur Cantonment**
25,000 Afghans attacked Roberts' forces
- Afghan losses: ~3,000 killed
- British losses: 5 killed, 28 wounded
- **Result**: Afghan defeat
:::

:::details **24 Dec 1879 - Reinforcements**
Brig-Gen Gough arrived with 2,700 reinforcements
:::

### Final Phase & Settlement (1880-1881)

:::details **Mar 1880 - Abdur Rahman Returns**
Abdur Rahman returned from Russian Turkestan to northern Afghanistan
:::

:::details **29 Mar 1880 - Stewart's March**
Gen Stewart marched from Kandahar to Kabul (7,249 men, 7,273 camp followers)
:::

:::details **19 Apr 1880 - Battle of Ahmed Khel**
Stewart defeated Ghilzais
- British: 17 killed, 124 wounded
- Afghans: ~1,200 casualties
:::

:::details **5 May 1880 - Stewart at Kabul**
Stewart arrived at Kabul, assumed overall command
:::

:::details **13 July 1880 - Mutiny**
Wali of Kandahar's army mutinied, joined Ayub Khan
:::

:::details **22 July 1880 - Abdur Rahman Recognized**
British recognized Abdur Rahman as Amir of Kabul
:::

:::details **27 July 1880 - BATTLE OF MAIWAND**
British disaster:
- Brig-Gen Burrows' 2,500 vs Ayub Khan's 15,000
- **British losses**: 971 killed, 168 wounded/missing
- Afghans victorious
:::

:::details **28 July 1880 - Withdrawal to Kandahar**
Gen Primrose withdrew into Kandahar citadel
:::

:::details **6 Aug 1880 - Failed Sortie**
Disastrous sortie from Kandahar: 99 killed, 109 wounded
:::

:::details **8-24 Aug 1880 - Kandahar Siege**
Yakub Khan besieged Kandahar
:::

:::details **11-31 Aug 1880 - ROBERTS' FAMOUS MARCH**
- **Kabul to Kandahar: 334 miles in 21 days**
- Force: ~10,000 men
- **Legendary feat** in military history
:::

:::details **1 Sept 1880 - Battle of Mazra**
Roberts defeated Yakub Khan's 20,000 near Kandahar
- British: 35 killed, 213 wounded
- Afghans: ~1,200 dead
:::

:::details **11 Sept 1880 - Kabul Evacuated**
Stewart evacuated Kabul
:::

:::details **15 Apr 1881 - British Withdrawal Complete**
Last British forces left Kandahar (turned over to Abdur Rahman 21 Apr)

## ‚öñÔ∏è Ilbert Bill Controversy (1883-1884)

### Background

:::details **2 Feb 1883 - Ilbert Bill Introduced**
Law Member **Sir Courtenay Ilbert** introduced bill: Indian Sessions Judge or District Magistrate could try British subjects on criminal charges
:::

### Opposition Erupts

:::details **28 Feb 1883 - Calcutta Opposition**
Calcutta Town Hall meeting opposed bill - racist speeches delivered
- European and Anglo-Indian Association formed
- Eurasian Association formed
:::

:::details **29 Mar 1883 - Defence Association**
**European and Anglo-Indian Defence Association** formed at Calcutta
- Anglo-Indian press (Englishman newspaper) published hysterical articles
- Focus: "Danger to European women under Indian judges"
- Opposition from: Calcutta bar, Bengal planters, non-official British
:::

:::details **9 Mar 1883 - Council Debate**
Imperial Legislative Council debated - insufficient support
:::

### Compromise

:::details **10 Aug 1883 - Turner Compromise**
Turner Compromise considered: limit jurisdiction, refer cases to High Courts
:::

:::details **21 Dec 1883 - Final Compromise**
- European defendant could claim jury with at least half European members
- No distinction between European/Indian magistrate assignment
- District Magistrate powers: up to 6 months imprisonment and/or Rs. 2,000 fine
:::

:::details **25 Jan 1884 - Bill Passed**
Ilbert Bill amended and passed
:::

## üëë Lord Curzon Era (1899-1905)

:::details **6 Jan 1899 - Lord Curzon**
Lord Curzon (1859-1925) assumed Viceroyalty
:::

### Persian Gulf Policy (1899)

:::details **Jan 1899 - Kuwait Agreement**
Curzon instructed Colonel Meade to conclude agreement with Sheikh Mubarak of Kuwait
- Preclude cession of land to foreign powers (especially Germany)
:::

:::details **16 Feb 1899 - Oman Threat**
British Admiral Douglas threatened Sultan of Oman
- Demanded termination of French coaling station agreement at Bunder Jissah
- Sultan complied under threat of Muscat bombardment
:::

:::details **16 Nov 1903 - Persian Gulf Tour**
Curzon toured Persian Gulf: Muscat, Sharjah, Bandar Abbas, Kuwait, Bushire
- Demonstrated British naval supremacy
- British Navy familiarized with Gulf ports
:::

### Famine Crisis (1900-1905)

:::details **1900 - Famine Impact**
Famine, cholera, plague impacted 60 million Indians
- Curzon became **first Viceroy to tour famine-stricken area**
- By spring 1900: Government providing 5 million with famine relief
:::

:::details **1901 - Famine Commission**
Famine Commission appointed (President: Sir Colin C. Scott-Moncrieff)
- Inaugurated irrigation projects: 6.5 million acres, cost ¬£30 million
:::

:::details **1896-1905 Total Impact**
- **Deaths**: ~8 million Indians (famine, cholera, plague)
- **Costs**:
  - Direct relief: ¬£6,670,000
  - Loans and advances: ¬£1,585,000
  - Land revenue remitted: ¬£1,333,000
  - Loans to Native States: ¬£1,800,000
  - Relief/lost revenue in Native States: ¬£4,000,000
:::

### Foreign Military Actions

:::details **13 June 1900 - Boxer Rebellion (China)**
- Indian Army sent ~3,000 troops to international force
- Units: 1st Sikhs, 24th Punjab Infantry, 7th Rajputs, Bengal Lancers
- **14 Aug 1900**: 1st Sikh Regiment helped raise 55-day siege of foreign legations at Peking
:::

:::details **Sept 1899 - South African War Support**
- Forwarded 13,200 British troops, 9,000 Indians (non-combatants)
- Lumsden's Horse (European Volunteer Contingent) raised
- Arrived in time to hold Natal for British
:::

:::details **1 Dec 1900 - Mahsud Tribe Blockade**
Mahsud tribe blockade for raids/robberies
- Passive blockade with punitive sallies
- **10 Mar 1902**: Temporary peace negotiated (lasted ~5 years)
:::

### Administrative Reforms

:::details **6 Feb 1901 - Victoria Memorial Hall**
Initiated at Calcutta Town Hall
- White marble structure as historical/national gallery
- Memorial to Queen Victoria, symbol of British Raj
- Curzon raised ¬£400,000 of ¬£5 million cost
- Designer: Sir William Emerson (1843-1924)
- Foundation stone: Jan 1906 (Prince of Wales)
- **Completed**: Dec 1921 (delayed by WWI)
:::

:::details **4 June 1901 - Imperial Cadet Corps**
Imperial Cadet Corps established
- Military training for elite young men from Princely/noble families
- First step toward Indian officers in Indian Army
- 1910: Under 20 members
:::

:::details **7 June 1901 - Indian Famine Union**
Indian Famine Union launched in London (Leonard Courtney)
- Study famine causes and remedies
- William Wedderburn's recommendations: elastic revenue collection, agriculture banks, "takav" loans
:::

:::details **25 July 1901 - Gandhi's Letter**
Curzon forwarded Gandhi's letter on Indian emigrants in South Africa
- Addressed indentured servitude conditions
- Later pressure from Gokhale, Hardinge, Rev. Andrews ended practice
:::

:::details **9 Nov 1901 - North-West Frontier Province**
North-West Frontier Province created from Punjab:
- Districts: Hazara, Peshawar, Kohat, Bannu, Dera Ismail Khan
- Agencies: Khyber, Malakand, Kurram, Tochi, Wana
- Reported directly to Viceroy
- First Chief Commissioner: Lt-Col Sir Harold A. Deane (1854-1908)
:::

:::details **Oct 1899 - Frontier Tribal Policy**
- Paid Pathans, Afridis, other tribes to police own territories
- Organized levies: Khyber Rifles, Samana Rifles, Kurram Militia
- British forces reduced by 11,000 to 4,000 at key centers
- Tribes received annual payment
:::

### Currency & Trade

:::details **20 Mar 1899 - Sugar Duties**
Act for countervailing duties on sugar
:::

:::details **Sept 1899 - Coinage and Currency Act**
Indian Coinage and Paper Currency Act (Sir Clinton Dawkins)
- Made sovereign legal tender at Rs. 15 = 1 sovereign
- Stabilized currency exchange
:::

:::details **1898 - Currency Committee**
Indian Currency Committee appointed (Chairman: Sir Henry Fowler)
:::

### Coronation Durbar (1901-1902)

:::details **29 Dec 1901-9 Jan 1902 - Coronation Durbar**
Coronation Durbar at Delhi (King Edward VII)
- Spectacular success despite criticism of expense
- **1 Jan 1902**: Honored 300+ Indian Mutiny veterans
:::

:::details **Mar 1902 - Hyderabad Agreement**
Agreement with Nizam of Hyderabad:
- British lease Berar perpetually for ¬£167,000 annual rent
- Nizam's sovereignty affirmed
- Hyderabad Contingent incorporated into Indian Army
- Nizam reduced irregular army, used rent to liquidate liabilities
:::

### Police & Security

:::details **1902 - Indian Police Commission**
Indian Police Commission (Chairman: Sir Andrew Fraser)
- Report (30 May 1903): Severely criticized police system
- Reforms: Greater efficiency, better training, eliminate corruption, increase pay
- Created **Department of Criminal Intelligence** (Director: Sir Harold A. Stuart)
:::

:::details **18 Feb 1898 - Penal Code Amendment**
Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Bill: Curbed sedition in vernacular press over plague/famine handling
:::

### Curzon-Kitchener Conflict (1902-1905)

:::details **28 Nov 1902 - Kitchener Arrives**
Lord Kitchener (1850-1916) arrived as Commander-in-Chief
:::

:::details **Feb 1903 - First Proposal**
Kitchener proposed reducing Military Member's powers
- Objected to 1861 Indian Councils Act command structure
- Curzon rejected, asked him to study system for a year
:::

:::details **May 1903 - Second Proposal**
Kitchener proposed Military Department under his orders
- Curzon rejected; Kitchener threatened resignation
:::

:::details **Apr 1904 - Bypassing Viceroy**
Kitchener submitted reorganization paper directly to Imperial Defence Committee (bypassing Viceroy)
:::

:::details **Sept 1904 - Resignation Threat**
Kitchener offered resignation to Acting Viceroy Lord Ampthill
- Convinced to withdraw
:::

:::details **12 Jan 1905 - Commission Suggestion**
St. John Brodrick (Secretary of State) suggested independent commission
:::

:::details **Apr 1905 - Press Campaign**
Kitchener organized press campaign in *The Times* and *Standard*
:::

:::details **30 May 1905 - Cabinet Decision**
Cabinet approved eliminating Military Member position
- Duties distributed to Commander-in-Chief and new Military Supply Member
:::

:::details **12 Aug 1905 - Curzon Resigned**
Curzon resigned over Military Supply Member selection dispute
- His recommendation (Gen. Sir Edmund Barrow) denied
:::

:::details **16 Aug 1905 - Resignation Accepted**
Balfour accepted Curzon's resignation
:::

:::details **21 Aug 1905 - Minto Selected**
Lord Minto (1845-1914) selected as successor
:::

:::details **19 Mar 1906 - Reorganization Implemented**
Kitchener's reorganization formally implemented
:::

### Tibet & Younghusband Mission (1903-1904)

:::details **Background**
Tensions over Tibetan missions to Czar, Russian agent Agvan Dorzhiev in Tibet, Dalai Lama refusing Curzon's letters
:::

:::details **8 Jan 1903 - Mission Urged**
Government urged forwarding British Mission to Lhasa
:::

:::details **18 July 1903 - Younghusband Arrives**
Brigadier-General Francis Younghusband (1863-1942) arrived at Khamba Jong with escort
- Negotiations failed over 5 months
:::

:::details **11 Dec 1903 - Mission Proceeds**
Mission proceeded to Gyantse with 2,000 Gurkhas (Brig-Gen James MacDonald)
:::

:::details **31 Mar 1904 - Encounter at Guru**
Encountered 2,000 Tibetan troops near Guru
- Firing: 840 Tibetans killed/wounded
:::

:::details **11 Apr 1904 - Gyantse Reached**
Reached Gyantse
- **5 May**: Attacked, fought off 240 Tibetan casualties
- **6 May**: Drove 3,000 Tibetans from Karo La Gorge
- **6 July**: Captured Tibetan fort at Gyantse
:::

:::details **3 Aug 1904 - Lhasa Entered**
Entered Lhasa unopposed (Dalai Lama fled)
:::

:::details **7 Sept 1904 - Treaty of Lhasa**
- British Trade Agent at Gyantse with right to visit Lhasa
- British occupation of Chumbi Valley
- Tibetan indemnity: Rs. 25 lakhs (75 years)
- London reduced to Rs. 25 lakhs (3 years); rebuke for exceeding instructions
:::

:::details **23 Sept 1904 - Younghusband Departs**
Younghusband left Lhasa
:::

:::details **Jan 1908 - Chumbi Valley Evacuated**
British evacuated Chumbi Valley after final indemnity payment
:::

### Bengal Partition Prelude (1903-1905)

:::details **7 Dec 1903 - Initial Planning**
Initial planning for Bengal partition
- Transfer Chittagong, Dacca, Mymensingh, Tippera Hills to Assam
- New province: East Bengal and Assam (Muslim majority)
- Religious vs ethnic basis
- 1904: Torrent of objections failed to move Curzon
:::

:::details **2 Feb 1905 - Proposals Submitted**
Proposals submitted to Secretary of State
:::

:::details **9 June 1905 - Approval**
Brodrick approved with minor modifications
:::

:::details **19 July 1905 - Resolution Passed**
Government formally passed partition resolution
:::

:::details **16 Oct 1905 - Partition Executed**
- East Bengal and Assam: 18 million Muslims, 12 million Hindus
- Provided Congress with major rallying issue
:::

### Other Initiatives

:::details **Mar 1904 - Cooperative Societies Act**
- Aid rural finance
- By 1911: 3,456 societies, capital ¬£686,000
:::

:::details **1903-05 - Persia-Afghanistan Boundary**
Sir Henry McMahon (1862-1949) settled Persia-Afghanistan boundary
:::

:::details **12 Dec 1904 - Afghan Treaty**
Sir Louis Dane negotiated new Afghan treaty
- **21 Mar 1905**: Concluded, reiterated prior agreements with Abdur Rahman
:::

:::details **Mar 1905 - Commerce and Industry Department**
Department of Commerce and Industry created
- Seat on Viceroy's Executive Council
- Activities: Railways, post/telegraph, ports, shipping, mining, customs
- Director: Sir John P. Hewett (1854-1941)
:::

:::details **1905 - Imperial Customs Service**
Imperial Customs Service created
- Regularized officer appointments, increased expertise
- Praised by business community
:::

:::details **15 Nov 1905 - Curzon Departs**
Curzon left India after 90-day extension to receive Prince and Princess of Wales
:::
