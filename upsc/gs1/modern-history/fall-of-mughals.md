## üîç Causes of Decline

:::details **Political Causes**
- **Weak Successors**: After Aurangzeb
- **War of Succession**: Constant conflicts
- **Jagirdari Crisis**: Revenue system collapsed
- **Mansabdari System**: Became ineffective
- **Central Authority**: Weakened progressively
:::

:::details **Economic Causes**
- **Empty Treasury**: Wars depleted
- **Expensive Wars**: Deccan campaigns
- **Jagir System**: Revenue declined
- **Trade Disruption**: European competition
- **Agricultural Crisis**: Peasant exploitation
:::

:::details **Military Causes**
- **Outdated Army**: Technology backward
- **Lack of Navy**: No maritime power
- **Mercenary Forces**: Unreliable troops
- **Maratha Tactics**: Guerrilla warfare
- **European Superiority**: Better weapons
:::

:::details **Administrative Causes**
- **Corruption Rampant**: Officials dishonest
- **Provincial Autonomy**: Governors independent
- **Inefficient Bureaucracy**: System collapsed
- **Communication Gap**: Vast empire
- **Local Rebellions**: Frequent uprisings
:::

:::details **Religious Causes**
- **Aurangzeb's Policies**: Alienated Hindus
- **Jizya Reimposed**: 1679 CE
- **Temple Destruction**: Hindu resentment
- **Rajput Alienation**: Lost support
- **Sikh Hostility**: Guru executions
:::

## üëë Later Mughals

:::details **Bahadur Shah I (1707-1712)**
- **Also Called**: Shah Alam I
- **Rajput Policy**: Conciliatory approach
- **Sikh Conflict**: Banda Bahadur
- **Maratha Relations**: Granted Sardeshmukhi
- **Death**: 1712 Lahore
:::

:::details **Jahandar Shah (1712-1713)**
- **Shortest Reign**: 11 months
- **Zulfiqar Khan**: Wazir dominated
- **Abolished Jizya**: Temporarily removed
- **Defeated By**: Farrukhsiyar quickly
:::

:::details **Farrukhsiyar (1713-1719)**
- **Sayyid Brothers**: Kingmakers powerful
- **British Favors**: Granted trading rights
- **Blinded & Killed**: By Sayyid Brothers
- **Weak Ruler**: Puppet emperor
:::

:::details **Muhammad Shah (1719-1748)**
- **Longest Reign**: 29 years
- **Nadir Shah**: 1739 invasion
- **Peacock Throne**: Looted away
- **Kohinoor Diamond**: Taken away
- **Weak Administration**: Empire declined
:::

:::details **Ahmad Shah (1748-1754)**
- **Wazir Safdarjung**: Dominated completely
- **Ahmad Shah Abdali**: Invasions began
- **Maratha Expansion**: Unchecked growth
- **Deposed**: By Alamgir II
:::

:::details **Alamgir II (1754-1759)**
- **Imad-ul-Mulk**: Wazir controlled
- **Maratha Influence**: Delhi occupied
- **Murdered**: By Wazir
- **Weak Emperor**: Nominal authority
:::

:::details **Shah Alam II (1759-1806)**
- **Battle of Buxar**: 1764 defeat
- **Treaty of Allahabad**: 1765 signed
- **Blinded**: By Ghulam Qadir
- **British Pension**: Dependent completely
:::

:::details **Akbar II (1806-1837)**
- **Ram Mohan Roy**: Raja title
- **British Control**: Complete dependence
- **Nominal Authority**: Only Delhi
:::

:::details **Bahadur Shah II (1837-1857)**
- **Last Mughal**: Emperor final
- **1857 Revolt**: Nominal leader
- **Exiled**: Rangoon Burma
- **Died**: 1862 exile
:::
