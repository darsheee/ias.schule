# Vedic Age
## Early & Later Vedic Period (1500 BCE - 600 BCE)

---

## üìÖ Timeline

:::details **Period Division**
- **Early Vedic**: 1500-1000 BCE
- **Later Vedic**: 1000-600 BCE
- **Total Duration**: ~900 years
- **Region**: Indo-Gangetic plains
:::

---

## üìö Literary Sources

### Early Vedic Period

:::details **Rigveda**
- **Oldest**: Vedic text known
- **Composition**: 1500-1000 BCE
- **Hymns**: 1028 suktas total
- **Mandalas**: 10 books divided
- **Language**: Vedic Sanskrit used
- **Gods**: Indra, Agni, Varuna
- **Family Books**: 2-7 oldest
:::

:::details **Rivers in Rigveda**
- **Sapta Sindhu**: Seven rivers mentioned
- **Saraswati**: Most sacred river
- **Indus**: Called Sindhu river
- **Five Rivers**: Punjab region
- **Yamuna**: Eastern boundary marked
:::

### Later Vedic Period

:::details **Samaveda**
- **Type**: Musical hymns collection
- **Borrowed**: From Rigveda mostly
- **Priests**: Udgatar chanted these
- **Purpose**: Soma sacrifice rituals
:::

:::details **Yajurveda**
- **Type**: Sacrificial formulas collection
- **Prose**: And verse mix
- **Versions**: Shukla, Krishna Yajurveda
- **Priests**: Adhvaryu used these
:::

:::details **Atharvaveda**
- **Type**: Magic spells collection
- **Content**: Charms, incantations included
- **Popular**: Religion reflected here
- **Later**: Addition to Vedas
:::

:::details **Brahmanas**
- **Type**: Prose texts commentary
- **Content**: Ritual explanations detailed
- **Priests**: Dominated these texts
- **Philosophy**: Karma concept introduced
:::

:::details **Aranyakas**
- **Meaning**: Forest books literally
- **Content**: Mystical, philosophical texts
- **Transition**: To Upanishads stage
:::

:::details **Upanishads**
- **Number**: 108 total traditionally
- **Philosophy**: Vedanta philosophy foundation
- **Concepts**: Brahman, Atman introduced
- **Knowledge**: Jnana emphasized over
:::

## üë• Social Structure

### Early Vedic Period

:::details **Family System**
- **Type**: Patriarchal system dominant
- **Joint**: Families lived together
- **Head**: Father controlled family
- **Griha-pati**: Household head called
:::

:::details **Varna System (Flexible)**
- **Brahmana**: Priests, scholars mainly
- **Kshatriya**: Warriors, rulers class
- **Vaishya**: Traders, farmers, herders
- **Shudra**: Servants, laborers mainly
- **Mobility**: Possible between varnas
- **Birth**: Not strictly fixed
:::

:::details **Status of Women**
- **Education**: Women received education
- **Upanayana**: Girls performed ceremony
- **Choice**: Marriage partner chosen
- **Scholars**: Gargi, Maitreyi, Lopamudra
- **Sabha**: Attended tribal assemblies
- **Remarriage**: Widow remarriage allowed
:::

:::details **Tribes & Clans**
- **Jana**: Tribe basic unit
- **Vis**: Clan subdivision unit
- **Grama**: Village smallest unit
- **Kula**: Family unit called
:::

### Later Vedic Period

:::details **Varna System (Rigid)**
- **Hereditary**: Birth-based system became
- **Brahmana**: Top position consolidated
- **Kshatriya**: Second position held
- **Vaishya**: Third rank assigned
- **Shudra**: Lowest position fixed
- **Mobility**: Became difficult now
:::

:::details **Status of Women (Declined)**
- **Education**: Declined for women
- **Upanayana**: Stopped for girls
- **Child Marriage**: Started emerging gradually
- **Remarriage**: Widow remarriage discouraged
- **Property**: Rights reduced significantly
- **Patriarchy**: Strengthened considerably more
:::

:::details **Marriage Types**
- **Eight Forms**: Mentioned in texts
- **Brahma**: Ideal form considered
- **Asura**: Bride-price payment involved
- **Gandharva**: Love marriage type
- **Rakshasa**: Capture by force
:::

## üí∞ Economic Life

### Early Vedic Period

:::details **Occupation**
- **Pastoralism**: Main occupation practiced
- **Cattle**: Wealth measure standard
- **Agriculture**: Secondary occupation only
- **Hunting**: Still practiced regularly
:::

:::details **Cattle Importance**
- **Wealth**: Measured in cattle
- **War**: Fought for cattle
- **Gavishti**: War literally "cow-quest"
- **Gotra**: Clan literally "cow-pen"
- **Duhitri**: Daughter literally milked
:::

:::details **Agriculture**
- **Yava**: Barley main crop
- **Godhuma**: Wheat also grown
- **Rice**: Not mentioned initially
- **Plough**: Wooden used regularly
- **Oxen**: Drew the plough
:::

:::details **Crafts**
- **Carpenter**: Skilled wood workers
- **Blacksmith**: Metal workers important
- **Potter**: Made earthenware vessels
- **Weaver**: Cotton, wool weavers
- **Chariot-maker**: Specialized craftsmen existed
:::

### Later Vedic Period

:::details **Agriculture Dominant**
- **Iron**: Ploughshare used now
- **Rice**: Cultivation started extensively
- **Wheat**: Widely grown crop
- **Barley**: Still cultivated regularly
- **Surplus**: Production increased significantly
:::

:::details **Crafts Specialization**
- **Iron**: Metallurgy developed advanced
- **Tools**: Agricultural tools improved
- **Weapons**: Better weapons manufactured
- **Pottery**: Painted Grey Ware
- **Guilds**: Craft guilds emerged
:::

:::details **Trade & Commerce**
- **Barter**: System still prevalent
- **Nishka**: Gold ornament currency
- **Long-distance**: Trade routes developed
- **Markets**: Urban centers emerged
:::

## üèõÔ∏è Political Organization

### Early Vedic Period

:::details **Tribal Assembly**
- **Sabha**: Council of elders
- **Samiti**: General tribal assembly
- **Vidatha**: Earliest assembly mentioned
- **Women**: Participated in assemblies
:::

:::details **Tribal Chief (Rajan)**
- **Election**: Chosen by assembly
- **Non-hereditary**: Position not fixed
- **War**: Leader in battles
- **Protector**: Of tribe people
- **Limited**: Powers controlled assembly
:::

:::details **Officials**
- **Purohita**: Chief priest advisor
- **Senani**: Army commander leader
- **Gramani**: Village headman chief
:::

### Later Vedic Period

:::details **Territorial Kingdom**
- **Janapada**: Territory-based kingdom now
- **Hereditary**: Kingship became fixed
- **Monarchy**: Absolute power increased
- **Divine**: Right claimed gradually
:::

:::details **Rituals for Kingship**
- **Rajasuya**: Consecration ceremony performed
- **Ashvamedha**: Horse sacrifice imperial
- **Vajapeya**: Chariot race ritual
:::

:::details **Officials Expanded**
- **Purohita**: Chief priest important
- **Senani**: Army general commander
- **Suta**: Charioteer, bard combined
- **Gramani**: Village headman administrator
- **Bhagadugha**: Tax collector officer
- **12 Ratnis**: Royal officials total
:::

:::details **Taxation**
- **Bali**: Voluntary tribute given
- **Tax**: 1/6 produce collected
- **Regular**: System developed gradually
:::

## üõê Religion

### Early Vedic Period

:::details **Nature Worship**
- **Indra**: War, thunder god
- **Agni**: Fire god important
- **Varuna**: Sky, water god
- **Soma**: Plant, drink deity
- **Surya**: Sun god worshipped
- **Ushas**: Dawn goddess depicted
:::

:::details **Worship Methods**
- **Yajna**: Sacrifices performed regularly
- **Mantras**: Hymns chanted loudly
- **Simple**: Rituals not complex
- **Outdoor**: No temples built
- **Priests**: Not dominant yet
:::

:::details **Important Gods**
- **Indra**: 250 hymns dedicated
- **Agni**: 200 hymns dedicated
- **Soma**: Intoxicating drink plant
- **Varuna**: Cosmic order maintainer
:::

### Later Vedic Period

:::details **Ritualism Increased**
- **Complex**: Sacrifices became elaborate
- **Brahmanas**: Priests dominated completely
- **Yajnas**: Expensive, lengthy ceremonies
- **Ashvamedha**: Imperial horse sacrifice
- **Rajasuya**: King consecration ritual
:::

:::details **New Gods**
- **Prajapati**: Creator god emerged
- **Vishnu**: Protector god important
- **Rudra**: Storm god (proto-Shiva)
- **Old Gods**: Declined in importance
:::

:::details **Philosophical Concepts**
- **Brahman**: Universal soul concept
- **Atman**: Individual soul concept
- **Karma**: Action, consequence doctrine
- **Moksha**: Liberation goal introduced
- **Rebirth**: Cycle of births
- **Monism**: One reality philosophy
:::

:::details **Upanishadic Philosophy**
- **Knowledge**: Path to liberation
- **Meditation**: Emphasized over rituals
- **Brahman-Atman**: Unity taught firmly
- **Maya**: Illusion concept introduced
- **Jnana**: Knowledge supreme path
:::
