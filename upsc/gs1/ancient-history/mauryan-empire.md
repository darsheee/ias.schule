## üëë Chandragupta Maurya (321-297 BCE)

:::details **Rise to Power**
- **Chanakya**: Guided him always
- **Nanda**: Overthrew last ruler
- **Magadha**: Conquered first kingdom
- **Age**: Young when started
- **Vision**: Pan-Indian empire planned
:::

:::details **Conquests**
- **Northwest**: Conquered territories extensively
- **Seleucus**: Defeated Greek ruler
- **Treaty**: Marriage alliance made
- **Territory**: Gained Afghanistan, Baluchistan
- **Southern**: Conquered till Karnataka
:::

:::details **Battle with Seleucus**
- **Year**: 305 BCE
- **Victory**: Chandragupta won decisively
- **Treaty**: Peace agreement signed
- **Gains**: 500 elephants given
- **Territory**: Afghanistan, Baluchistan gained
- **Ambassador**: Megasthenes sent here
:::

:::details **Abdication**
- **Religion**: Embraced Jainism finally
- **Guru**: Bhadrabahu Jain monk
- **Sravanabelagola**: Moved to Karnataka
- **Death**: Starvation (Sallekhana) chosen
:::

## üëë Ashoka (268-232 BCE)

### Early Life

:::details **Succession**
- **War**: Brothers killed brutally
- **Wait**: 4 years coronation
- **Cruel**: Initially very violent
- **Nickname**: Chandashoka (fierce Ashoka)
:::

:::details **Kalinga War (261 BCE)**
- **Location**: Modern Odisha region
- **Casualties**: 100,000 killed brutally
- **Captives**: 150,000 taken forcibly
- **Impact**: Ashoka transformed completely
- **Regret**: Expressed deep remorse
- **Turning**: Point in life
:::

### Dhamma Policy

:::details **Ashoka's Dhamma**
- **Meaning**: Righteousness, moral principles
- **Not**: Buddhism religion exclusively
- **Universal**: Values for all
- **Tolerance**: Religious harmony promoted
- **Non-violence**: Ahimsa emphasized strongly
- **Respect**: Elders, parents, teachers
:::

:::details **Dhamma Principles**
- **Non-violence**: Towards all beings
- **Respect**: Parents, elders always
- **Tolerance**: All religions equally
- **Charity**: Helping poor people
- **Truth**: Speaking always honestly
- **Compassion**: Towards all creatures
:::

:::details **Dhamma Propagation**
- **Edicts**: Inscribed on rocks
- **Officers**: Dhamma Mahamatras appointed
- **Missions**: Sent abroad widely
- **Practice**: Led by example
:::

### Edicts

:::details **Types of Edicts**
- **Major Rock**: 14 edicts total
- **Minor Rock**: Shorter inscriptions
- **Pillar**: 7 major edicts
- **Cave**: Inscriptions in caves
:::

:::details **Languages Used**
- **Prakrit**: Common language mostly
- **Brahmi**: Script predominantly used
- **Kharosthi**: Northwest region only
- **Greek**: Afghanistan region inscriptions
- **Aramaic**: Some Afghan inscriptions
:::

:::details **Important Edicts**
- **Rock Edict I**: Animal killing banned
- **Rock Edict XII**: Religious tolerance preached
- **Rock Edict XIII**: Kalinga war remorse
- **Pillar Edict VII**: Dhamma principles explained
:::

:::details **Pillar Inscriptions**
- **Number**: Seven major edicts
- **Locations**: Scattered across empire
- **Height**: 40-50 feet tall
- **Material**: Polished sandstone used
- **Capital**: Animal sculptures topped
- **Lion**: Capital most famous
:::

:::details **Lion Capital (Sarnath)**
- **Location**: Sarnath, Varanasi near
- **Lions**: Four back-to-back
- **Abacus**: Wheel, animals carved
- **Chakra**: Dharma wheel prominent
- **National**: Emblem of India
:::

### Buddhist Missions

:::details **Domestic Missions**
- **Kashmir**: Majjhantika sent there
- **Gandhara**: Mahadeva sent there
- **Mysore**: Rakkhita sent there
- **Maharashtra**: Dharmarakkhita sent there
:::

:::details **Foreign Missions**
- **Sri Lanka**: Mahinda, Sanghamitta
- **Burma**: Sona, Uttara sent
- **Greece**: Dharmarakkhita sent there
- **Egypt**: Rakkhita sent there
- **Syria**: Missions sent successfully
:::

:::details **Sri Lanka Mission**
- **Son**: Mahinda led mission
- **Daughter**: Sanghamitta accompanied him
- **King**: Tissa converted successfully
- **Bodhi**: Tree cutting sent
:::

### Administration

:::details **Central Administration**
- **Mauryan**: Emperor supreme authority
- **Council**: Ministers advised regularly
- **Bureaucracy**: Well-organized efficient system
- **Espionage**: Network maintained extensively
:::

:::details **Provincial Administration**
- **Provinces**: Five major divisions
- **Capitals**: Taxila, Ujjain, Tosali
- **Governors**: Princes appointed usually
- **Kumara**: Prince as governor
:::

:::details **District Administration**
- **District**: Administrative unit smaller
- **Officers**: Rajukas appointed regularly
- **Revenue**: Collection managed efficiently
- **Justice**: Delivered at level
:::

:::details **Municipal Administration**
- **Committees**: Six boards managed
- **Industries**: Regulated by boards
- **Foreigners**: Separate board managed
- **Birth**: Deaths registered officially
:::

### Religious Policy

:::details **Tolerance**
- **All**: Religions respected equally
- **Donations**: Given to all
- **Brahmanas**: Received royal patronage
- **Jains**: Also supported generously
- **Ajivikas**: Cave donations given
:::

:::details **Buddhist Patronage**
- **Council**: Third organized successfully
- **Stupas**: 84,000 built reportedly
- **Viharas**: Monasteries constructed extensively
- **Sanchi**: Stupa most famous
:::
