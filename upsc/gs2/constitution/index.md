# Indian Constitution
## Framework, Evolution & Basic Structure

---

## üìú Constitutional Framework

:::details **Constituent Assembly**
- **Cabinet Mission Plan**: 1946 formation
- **Duration**: 2 years 11 months 18 days
- **Members**: 389 members total
- **Chairman**: Dr. Rajendra Prasad
- **Drafting Committee**: Dr. B.R. Ambedkar chairman
- **Sessions**: 11 sessions total
:::

:::details **Criticism of Constituent Assembly**
- **Not Directly Elected**: Indirect election process
- **Congress Dominated**: Single party majority
- **Lawyers Upper Castes**: Dominance evident
- **Limited Representation**: Women, minorities underrepresented
:::

:::details **Constitutional Evolution**
- **Government of India Act 1935**: Major influence
  - Federal structure adopted
  - Emergency provisions borrowed
  - Public service commissions
- **Previous Acts**: 1773, 1858, 1909, 1919
- **Foreign Constitutions**: UK, USA, Ireland, Canada
:::

## üéØ Significant Features

:::details **Form of Government**
- **Federal Structure**: Centre-State division
- **Parliamentary System**: Executive from legislature
- **Quasi-Federal**: Strong Centre bias
- **Bicameral Legislature**: Two Houses
:::

:::details **Written Constitution**
- **Longest**: Written constitution world
- **Original**: 395 Articles, 8 Schedules
- **Current**: 470+ Articles, 12 Schedules
- **Detailed**: Comprehensive provisions
:::

:::details **Fundamental Rights & Duties**
- **Part III**: Fundamental Rights
- **Justiciable**: Court enforceable
- **Part IVA**: Fundamental Duties
- **Non-Justiciable**: Moral obligations
:::

:::details **Directive Principles**
- **Part IV**: DPSP provisions
- **Non-Justiciable**: Not court enforceable
- **State Obligations**: Policy guidelines
- **Fundamental in Governance**: Article 37
:::

## ‚öñÔ∏è Basic Structure Doctrine

:::details **Kesavananda Bharati vs. State of Kerala (1973)**
- **13-Judge Bench**: Landmark judgment
- **Basic Structure**: Cannot be amended
- **Parliament Powers**: Limited power
- **Judicial Review**: Strengthened
:::

:::details **Key Elements of Basic Structure**
- **Supremacy**: Constitution supreme
- **Republican**: Democratic form
- **Secular**: Character protected
- **Federal**: Character maintained
- **Separation of Powers**: Maintained
- **Judicial Review**: Protected
- **Rule of Law**: Fundamental
- **Individual Dignity**: Freedom protected
:::

:::details **I.R. Coelho Case (2007)**
- **Ninth Schedule**: Subject to review
- **Basic Structure Test**: Applied
- **All Laws**: Can be reviewed
- **Post-Kesavananda**: Laws after 1973
:::

:::details **Recent Applications**
- **NJAC Act 2015**: Struck down
  - Violated judicial independence
  - Basic structure breached
- **Demonetization 2018**: Upheld
  - Doesn't violate basic structure
  - Economic policy matter
:::

:::details **Criticism of Basic Structure**
- **No Textual Basis**: Not in Constitution
- **Judicial Overreach**: Legislative domain
- **Subjective Application**: Case by case
- **Undemocratic**: Unelected judges decide
- **Counter-Majoritarian**: Limits Parliament
:::

## üìã Constitutional Amendments

:::details **Amendment Process (Article 368)**
- **Simple Majority**: Some provisions
- **Special Majority**: 2/3rd present, absolute majority
- **Ratification**: Half states approval required
- **No Time Limit**: Ratification process
:::

:::details **Important Amendments**
- **1st Amendment (1951)**: Land reforms, speech restrictions
- **7th Amendment (1956)**: States reorganization
- **24th Amendment (1971)**: Parliament can amend FRs
- **42nd Amendment (1976)**: Mini Constitution
- **44th Amendment (1978)**: Restored balance
- **73rd/74th (1992)**: Panchayati Raj, municipalities
- **86th Amendment (2002)**: Right to Education
- **99th Amendment (2014)**: NJAC (struck down)
- **101st Amendment (2016)**: GST implementation
- **103rd Amendment (2019)**: EWS reservation
:::
