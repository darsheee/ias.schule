# Minorities & Religious Freedom
## Constitutional Safeguards & Challenges

---

## üìú Constitutional Provisions

:::details **Religious Freedom (Articles 25-28)**
- **Art 25**: Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice and propagation of religion
  - Subject to public order, morality and health
  - State can regulate secular activities associated with religion
- **Art 26**: Freedom to manage religious affairs
  - Establish and maintain institutions
  - Own and acquire movable and immovable property
  - Administer property in accordance with law
- **Art 27**: No person compelled to pay taxes for promotion of any religion
- **Art 28**: No religious instruction in government educational institutions
:::

:::details **Cultural & Educational Rights (Articles 29-30)**
- **Art 29**: Protection of language, script and culture
  - Any section of citizens with distinct culture can conserve it
  - No denial of admission in state-aided institutions on grounds of religion, race, caste
- **Art 30**: Right to establish and administer educational institutions
  - Religious and linguistic minorities
  - State aid shall not discriminate
  - Compulsory acquisition must be compensated reasonably
:::

:::details **Other Safeguards**
- **Art 15**: No discrimination on grounds of religion
- **Art 16**: Equality of opportunity in public employment
- **Art 350A**: Instruction in mother tongue at primary stage
- **Art 350B**: Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities
- **Art 347**: Special provision for language in a state
- **Preamble**: Secular character of Indian state
:::

## üèõÔ∏è Institutional Framework

:::details **National Commission for Minorities**
- **Established**: 1992 (statutory status 1993)
- **Constitutional Status**: Demanded but not yet granted
- **Functions**:
  - Evaluate progress of minorities' development
  - Monitor safeguards in Constitution and laws
  - Investigate complaints of deprivation
  - Study problems and recommend measures
  - Periodic reports to government
:::

:::details **Notified Minorities**
- **Six Communities**: Muslims, Christians, Sikhs, Buddhists, Parsis, Jains
- **Jains**: Added in 2014 (after long demand)
- **State-Specific**: Different minorities in different states
- **Criteria**: Numeric minority, distinct culture/language/script
- **Debate**: Jews, Bahais, other communities not included
:::

:::details **National Commission for Minority Educational Institutions (NCMEI)**
- **Established**: 2004
- **Functions**:
  - Advise on policy for minority institutions
  - Recommend measures for protection
  - Inquire into grievances
  - Recommend amendments in laws
- **Quasi-Judicial**: Powers to settle disputes
:::

:::details **Special Officer for Linguistic Minorities (Art 350B)**
- **Appointment**: By President
- **Duties**: 
  - Investigate matters relating to safeguards
  - Report to President at intervals specified
  - President places reports before Parliament
- **Powers**: Limited, advisory in nature
:::

## üìä Socio-Economic Challenges

:::details **Sachar Committee Findings (2006)**
- **Education**: Muslims lagging in literacy and enrollment
  - Literacy rate below national average
  - Low representation in higher education
  - School dropout rates high
- **Employment**: 
  - Underrepresentation in government jobs
  - Concentration in informal sector
  - Low access to credit for self-employment
- **Economic**: 
  - Living conditions poor in many areas
  - Share of public employment very low
  - Access to bank credit difficult
:::

:::details **Ranganath Mishra Commission (2007)**
- **Key Recommendations**:
  - 15% reservation for Muslims in education and employment
  - Equitable share in economic activities
  - Implement Sachar Committee recommendations
  - Establish Equal Opportunity Commission
- **Status**: Mostly not implemented
- **Controversy**: Reservation on religious grounds debated
:::

:::details **Current Disparities**
- **Representation**: Low in legislatures, judiciary, bureaucracy
- **Educational Institutions**: Minority institutions face regulatory issues
- **Hate Crimes**: Rising incidents of communal violence
- **Ghettoization**: Concentration in specific areas
- **Political Participation**: Limited voice in policy-making
- **Access to Justice**: Legal aid and awareness gap
:::

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Key Issues & Concerns

:::details **Communal Violence**
- **Incidents**: Periodic riots and targeted violence
- **Lack of Prevention**: No comprehensive law
- **Impunity**: Slow justice for victims
- **Hate Speech**: Growing instances online and offline
- **Mob Lynching**: Recent phenomenon of vigilantism
- **Need**: Communal Violence Bill pending for years
:::

:::details **Religious Conversion Laws**
- **State Laws**: Several states enacted freedom of religion acts
- **Anti-Conversion**: Restrict conversion through allurement/force
- **Controversy**: 
  - Violate Art 25 propagation right
  - Misuse against inter-faith marriages
  - Prior permission requirements problematic
- **Supreme Court**: Yet to rule comprehensively
:::

:::details **Triple Talaq & Personal Laws**
- **Shayara Bano (2017)**: Triple talaq struck down as unconstitutional
- **Criminal Law (2019)**: Triple talaq made criminal offense
- **Debate**: Gender justice vs religious autonomy
- **Other Practices**: Polygamy, inheritance laws contentious
- **UCC Demand**: Uniform Civil Code (Art 44) debate ongoing
:::

:::details **Places of Worship Act, 1991**
- **Provision**: Religious character as on 15 August 1947 to be maintained
- **Ayodhya Exception**: Specifically excluded
- **Recent Challenges**: Gyanvapi, Mathura cases
- **Controversy**: Historical vs current religious character
- **Supreme Court**: Examining validity and scope
:::

:::details **Minority Educational Institutions**
- **T.M.A. Pai Case (2002)**: Right to establish and administer institutions
- **Regulatory Balance**: Autonomy vs maintaining standards
- **Reservation**: Whether applicable in minority institutions
- **Aided vs Unaided**: Different regulatory frameworks
- **Challenges**: Over-regulation complaints
:::

## üèõÔ∏è Supreme Court Position on Secularism

:::details **Key Judgments**
- **S.R. Bommai (1994)**: 
  - Secularism is basic structure of Constitution
  - Religion and politics must be kept separate
  - State power cannot be used for religious purposes
- **Ismail Faruqui (1994)**: 
  - Mosque not essential for namaz
  - Religious character can be ascertained
- **Kesavananda Bharati (1973)**: 
  - Secular character part of basic structure
  - Cannot be amended by Parliament
:::

:::details **Recent Developments**
- **Sabarimala (2018)**: Entry of women upheld
  - Religious practice vs gender equality
  - Essential religious practice test
- **Ayodhya (2019)**: Ram Mandir-Babri Masjid dispute
  - Archaeological evidence considered
  - Alternative land for mosque
- **Article 370 (2019)**: Special status to J&K removed
  - Religious freedom implications under examination
:::
