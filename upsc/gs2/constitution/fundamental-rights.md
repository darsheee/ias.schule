## 1Ô∏è‚É£ Right to Equality (Articles 14-18)

:::details **Article 14: Equality Before Law**
- **Equal**: Protection under laws
- **All**: Citizens, non-citizens included
- **Negative**: Concept prohibits discrimination
- **British**: Origin rule of
- **Exception**: President, Governors immune
:::

:::details **Article 15: Prohibition of Discrimination**
- **Grounds**: Religion, race, caste
- **Sex**: Place of birth
- **No**: Citizen discriminated against
- **Exception**: Women, children provisions
- **Public**: Places accessible equally
:::

:::details **Article 16: Equality in Public Employment**
- **Equal**: Opportunity in employment
- **State**: Cannot discriminate citizens
- **Reservation**: Allowed for SC/ST
- **Exception**: Religious institutions exempt
- **Domicile**: State can prescribe
:::

:::details **Article 17: Abolition of Untouchability**
- **Abolished**: Untouchability practice completely
- **Offense**: Punishable by law
- **Act**: Protection of Civil Rights
- **Social**: Evil eliminated constitutionally
:::

:::details **Article 18: Abolition of Titles**
- **Military**: Educational titles allowed
- **Foreign**: Titles not accepted
- **No**: Titles except above
- **Bharat**: Ratna not title
- **Exception**: Academic, military only
:::

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ Right to Freedom (Articles 19-22)

:::details **Article 19: Six Freedoms**
- **Speech**: Expression freedom granted
- **Assembly**: Peaceful gathering allowed
- **Association**: Form associations freely
- **Movement**: Anywhere in India
- **Residence**: Live anywhere chosen
- **Profession**: Any occupation practice
:::

:::details **Article 19(1)(a): Freedom of Speech**
- **Includes**: Press freedom implicitly
- **Media**: Expression all forms
- **Internet**: Access considered included
- **Not**: Absolute freedom always
- **Restrictions**: Reasonable grounds allowed
:::

:::details **Restrictions on Speech (Article 19(2))**
- **Sovereignty**: Integrity of India
- **Security**: Of the state
- **Public**: Order maintenance required
- **Decency**: Morality standards maintained
- **Contempt**: Of court prevented
- **Defamation**: False statements prohibited
:::

:::details **Article 20: Protection from Conviction**
- **Ex-post-facto**: Law not applied
- **Double**: Jeopardy prohibited completely
- **Self**: Incrimination not forced
:::

:::details **Article 21: Right to Life**
- **Life**: Personal liberty included
- **Most**: Important fundamental right
- **Expanded**: Through judicial interpretation
- **Due**: Process must follow
- **Cannot**: Be suspended ever
:::

:::details **Article 21 Expanded Rights**
- **Privacy**: Right upheld 2017
- **Education**: Right to education
- **Environment**: Pollution-free environment required
- **Speedy**: Trial right included
- **Legal**: Aid provided free
- **Health**: Right to health
:::

:::details **Article 21A: Right to Education**
- **Age**: 6-14 years children
- **Free**: Compulsory education provided
- **Added**: 86th Amendment 2002
- **RTE**: Act 2009 implemented
:::

:::details **Article 22: Protection from Arrest**
- **Informed**: Grounds of arrest
- **Lawyer**: Consult right immediately
- **24 hours**: Produced before magistrate
- **Preventive**: Detention laws allowed
- **NDPS**: NSA exceptions exist
:::

## 6Ô∏è‚É£ Right to Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

:::details **Article 32: Enforcement of Rights**
- **Dr. Ambedkar**: Called heart, soul
- **Supreme**: Court can approach
- **Writs**: Five types issued
- **Suspended**: Only during emergency
- **Most**: Important fundamental right
:::

:::details **Five Writs**
- **Habeas Corpus**: Produce the body
- **Mandamus**: Command to duty
- **Prohibition**: Stop lower court
- **Certiorari**: Quash order issued
- **Quo Warranto**: Challenge authority held
:::

:::details **Habeas Corpus**
- **Meaning**: Produce the body
- **Illegal**: Detention challenged successfully
- **Liberty**: Protection ensures always
- **Court**: Orders release immediately
:::

:::details **Mandamus**
- **Meaning**: We command order
- **Public**: Duty performance ordered
- **Authority**: Compelled to act
- **Cannot**: Be issued against
:::

:::details **Prohibition**
- **Higher**: To lower court
- **Exceed**: Jurisdiction cases stopped
- **Issued**: During proceedings ongoing
- **Preventive**: Nature of writ
:::

:::details **Certiorari**
- **Quash**: Orders of tribunals
- **After**: Order passed already
- **Curative**: Nature of writ
- **Judicial**: Review mechanism important
:::

:::details **Quo Warranto**
- **Meaning**: By what authority
- **Public**: Office holder challenged
- **Qualifications**: Verified through court
- **Ouster**: From office possible
:::

## üìä Important Amendments

:::details **1st Amendment (1951)**
- **Article 19**: Restrictions strengthened significantly
- **Zamindari**: Abolition protected legally
- **Reasonable**: Restrictions expanded scope
:::

:::details **24th Amendment (1971)**
- **Parliament**: Can amend rights
- **Article 13**: Amended significantly now
- **Article 368**: Not covered under
:::

:::details **25th Amendment (1971)**
- **Property**: Right diluted considerably
- **Compensation**: Amount not justiciable
- **Directive**: Principles priority given
:::

:::details **42nd Amendment (1976)**
- **Ten**: Words added Preamble
- **Fundamental**: Duties added new
- **Article 32**: Judicial review limited
- **Reversed**: 43rd Amendment partially
:::

:::details **44th Amendment (1978)**
- **Property**: Right removed completely
- **Article 19**: Restored earlier position
- **Article 20**: 21 protected
- **Emergency**: Provisions modified substantially
:::

:::details **86th Amendment (2002)**
- **Article 21A**: Education right added
- **6-14**: Years age group
- **Free**: Compulsory education provided
- **Article 45**: Deleted from DPSP
:::
