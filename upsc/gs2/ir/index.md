- **Extended Neighbourhood**: Beyond immediate South Asian neighbours

- **All-Weather Friend**: China-Pakistan relationship descriptor

- **Just-Fair-Equitable Global Order**: India's vision for multilateralism
- **Tier I and Tier II Diplomacy**: Levels of diplomatic engagement
- **Act East Policy**: India's Southeast Asia engagement
- **Debate-Discussion-Dissention**: Framework for border negotiations
- **Soft Diplomacy**: Cultural and people-to-people engagement
- **Regional Superpower/Leader**: India's aspiration in South Asia
- **Multipolar World**: Post-Cold War international system
- **Roti Beti ka Rishta**: India-Nepal civilizational ties
- **Multi-Alignment vs Non-Alignment**: India's evolving foreign policy
- **Space Diplomacy**: Satellite cooperation
- **China Card**: Leveraging China factor
- **Offensive Defence**: Proactive security posture
- **Panchsheel Doctrine**: Five principles of peaceful coexistence

- **Big Brother to Elder Brother**: Changing India's regional image
- **3C's: Commerce-Connectivity-Culture**: Pillars of engagement

## <a id="neighbourhood-first-policy"></a>üè† NEIGHBOURHOOD FIRST POLICY

:::details **Historical Evolution**
- **British Policy**: Used Nepal and Afghanistan as buffer states
- **Nehru's Panchsheel (1954)**: Five principles of peaceful coexistence
- **Gujaral Doctrine (1996)**: Non-reciprocity, no interference, peaceful dispute resolution, regional cooperation
- **Manmohan Doctrine**: Economic interdependence focus
- **Modi's Neighbourhood First**: Proactive engagement, development partnership
:::

:::details **4 Pillars**
1. Diplomatic and political priority to neighbourhood
2. Active support for development
3. Greater connectivity and integration
4. Promote India-led regionalism
:::

:::details **Key Achievements & Challenges**
**Achievements:**
- Land Boundary Agreement with Bangladesh (2015)
- Increased connectivity projects (BBIN, Kaladan)
- COVID-19 assistance (SAARC fund, vaccine diplomacy)
- Enhanced defence cooperation (joint exercises)

**Challenges:**
- Rising Chinese footprint (BRI projects)
- Border disputes (India-Nepal Kalapani, India-China LAC)
- SAARC paralysis (India-Pakistan tensions)
- Trust deficit among neighbours
- Trade barriers and low economic integration
:::
