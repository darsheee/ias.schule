## ðŸ‡µðŸ‡° INDIA-PAKISTAN RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction & Historical Context**
- **Partition Legacy**: 1947 division created fundamental tensions
- **Four Wars**: 1947-48, 1965, 1971, 1999 Kargil
- **Kashmir**: Core unresolved issue since independence
- **Nuclear Dimension**: Both declared nuclear states (1998)
- **Indus Waters Treaty**: 1960, survived all conflicts
:::

:::details **Key Issues in India-Pakistan Relations**
**Cross-Border Terrorism:**
- Pakistan-based terror groups: Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM)
- Major attacks: 26/11 Mumbai (2008), Pathankot (2016), Uri (2016), Pulwama (2019)
- Safe havens in Pakistan for terrorists
- UN-designated terrorists (Hafiz Saeed, Masood Azhar) roam free

**Kashmir Dispute:**
- Article 370 abrogation (2019) - India's internal matter
- Pakistan's internationalization attempts at UN, OIC
- Line of Control (LoC) ceasefire violations
- Pakistan-Occupied Kashmir (PoK) human rights issues

**Water Disputes:**
- Indus Waters Treaty tensions
- India's notice (2023) seeking treaty modifications
- Pakistan's objection to Indian hydropower projects
- Kishenganga and Ratle projects controversy

**Economic Relations:**
- MFN status revoked by India (2019 post-Pulwama)
- Trade volume: Only $629.46 million (2022)
- Pakistan blocks India's transit to Afghanistan
- Smuggling and informal trade significant
:::

:::details **Pakistan's Crumbling Economy - Impact on India**
**Economic Crisis Indicators:**
- GDP: $350 billion economy struggling
- External debt: Over $130 billion
- IMF bailouts: 23rd program sought
- Reserves: Less than 2 months of imports
- Inflation: Over 30% in recent months

**Impact on India:**
- **Refugee Concerns**: Economic migrants trying to cross border
- **Terrorism Financing**: Desperate economy may increase reliance on terror groups
- **Regional Instability**: Failed state scenario risks
- **Opportunities**: Trade diversion to India
- **China Factor**: Chinese debt trap diplomacy increasing
:::

:::details **India's Response Strategy**
**Surgical Strikes:**
- 2016: Post-Uri attack, surgical strikes across LoC
- 2019: Balakot airstrike post-Pulwama attack
- Message: India will not tolerate terrorism

**Diplomatic Isolation:**
- FATF grey list pressure on Pakistan
- Bilateral talks suspended until terror ends
- International forums highlighting Pakistan's terror support

**Economic Measures:**
- MFN status withdrawal
- Customs duty increases
- Restricted trade and connectivity
:::

:::details **Indus Waters Treaty - Recent Developments**
**Treaty Provisions:**
- Signed: 1960, World Bank mediation
- Eastern rivers to India: Sutlej, Beas, Ravi
- Western rivers to Pakistan: Indus, Jhelum, Chenab
- India gets 20% water, Pakistan 80%

**India's Notice (2023):**
- Seeking modifications citing changed circumstances
- Pakistan's non-cooperation in dispute resolution
- Terrorism and PoK issues linked
- Treaty survived despite wars, but under strain

**Issues:**
- Pakistan objects to run-of-river projects
- Delays in dispute resolution mechanisms
- Kishenganga, Ratle arbitrations pending
- Climate change affecting water availability
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
- **Terrorism First**: Pakistan must dismantle terror infrastructure
- **Track II Diplomacy**: Academic, cultural exchanges when possible
- **Economic Engagement**: Conditional on security improvements
- **Regional Forums**: Use SCO, SAARC for limited engagement
- **International Pressure**: Continue FATF, UN mechanisms
- **People-to-People**: Facilitate divided families, pilgrimage
:::

## ðŸ‡§ðŸ‡© INDIA-BANGLADESH RELATIONS

:::details **Significance of India-Bangladesh Relations**
**Historical Bonds:**
- 1971 Liberation War: India's crucial role in Bangladesh's independence
- 93,000 Pakistani soldiers surrendered to Indian forces
- Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's deep friendship with India
- Shared Bengali culture and language

**Strategic Importance:**
- Bangladesh is India's largest trading partner in South Asia
- Critical for Northeast connectivity
- Bay of Bengal maritime cooperation
- Buffer against China's influence in region
:::

:::details **Areas of Cooperation**
**Trade & Economy:**
- Bilateral trade: $18.2 billion (2021-22)
- India's 2nd biggest export destination in South Asia
- Major Indian exports: Cotton, vehicles, machinery, pharmaceuticals
- Lines of Credit: India provided $8 billion+

**Connectivity:**
- BBIN Initiative: Bangladesh-Bhutan-India-Nepal motor vehicles agreement
- Akhaura-Agartala rail link operational
- Inland waterway protocol: 8 routes operational
- Maitri Setu: India-Bangladesh friendship bridge

**Energy Cooperation:**
- 1160 MW power supply from India
- Cross-border electricity transmission
- Joint hydropower projects planned
- Renewable energy cooperation

**Defence & Security:**
- Joint military exercises: Sampriti
- Maritime cooperation in Bay of Bengal
- Intelligence sharing on terrorism
- India training Bangladesh military personnel

**Development Partnership:**
- India built Bangladesh Parliament building
- Healthcare: Cancer hospital, medical supplies
- Education: Scholarships for Bangladeshi students
- Quick Impact Projects in border areas
:::

:::details **Issues in Relationship**
**Teesta Water Sharing:**
- Agreement pending since 2011
- West Bengal CM's opposition blocking Centre
- Bangladesh seeks fair share during dry season
- Political issue in West Bengal state politics

**Illegal Migration:**
- Bangladeshi migrants in India, especially Assam
- NRC (National Register of Citizens) concerns
- CAA (Citizenship Amendment Act) impact
- Border management challenges

**Border Killings:**
- BSF-BGB tensions over cattle smuggling
- Civilian casualties at border
- Need for non-lethal weapons

**Trade Imbalance:**
- Heavily in India's favour
- Bangladesh seeks tariff reductions
- Non-tariff barriers on both sides
- Market access issues for Bangladesh products

**Rohingya Refugees:**
- Over 1 million refugees in Bangladesh
- Burden on Bangladesh economy
- India's concerns about infiltration
- Need for Myanmar solution
:::

:::details **Government Steps & Recent Developments**
**Land Boundary Agreement (2015):**
- Historic settlement of 68-year-old enclave issue
- 162 enclaves exchanged
- 51,000 people got citizenship choice
- International praise for peaceful resolution

**COVID-19 Cooperation:**
- India provided vaccines under Vaccine Maitri
- Medical supplies and PPE kits
- Health sector cooperation enhanced

**Defence Deals:**
- India providing patrol vessels
- Coastal surveillance radar systems
- Joint maritime exercises

**50th Anniversary (2021):**
- PM Modi's visit to Bangladesh
- Multiple agreements signed
- Cultural exchanges intensified
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
- **Teesta Agreement**: Centre-State coordination needed urgently
- **Enhanced Trade**: Reduce tariffs, facilitate border trade
- **Border Management**: Modern tech, reduce casualties
- **Connectivity**: Complete pending rail, road, waterway projects
- **Sub-Regional**: Leverage BIMSTEC and BBIN effectively
- **People-to-People**: Easier visas, cultural programs
- **Water Management**: All 54 common rivers need frameworks
- **Counter-Terrorism**: Continue joint operations against insurgents
:::

:::details **Conclusion**
India-Bangladesh relations represent one of India's most successful bilateral partnerships. Built on the foundation of the 1971 Liberation War, the relationship has matured into comprehensive cooperation across multiple sectors. While challenges like Teesta water sharing remain, the overall trajectory is positive with strong political will on both sides.
:::

## ðŸ‡³ðŸ‡µ INDIA-NEPAL RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction - Special Relationship**
- **Roti-Beti ka Rishta**: Deep civilizational connections
- **Open Border**: Since 1950, free movement without passports
- **Hindu-Buddhist Heritage**: Shared religious and cultural bonds
- **Gurkha Regiments**: In Indian Army since British era
- **1950 Treaty**: Peace and Friendship - special relationship framework
:::

:::details **India-Nepal Ties - Areas of Cooperation**
**Economic Relations:**
- India is Nepal's largest trading partner
- Trade volume: NPR 976.78 billion (2020-21)
- Indian investments in: Hydropower, manufacturing, services, tourism
- Remittances: Significant from Nepali workers in India
- Currency: Indian rupee freely circulates in Nepal

**Development Assistance:**
- Post-2015 earthquake: India's $1 billion+ reconstruction aid
- Infrastructure: Roads, bridges, hospitals, schools
- Small Development Projects: Over 470 projects
- Emergency assistance during disasters

**Connectivity:**
- Multiple border crossing points (28+)
- Rail links: Janakpur-Jaynagar, Jogbani-Biratnagar
- Road connectivity being expanded
- Air connectivity: Multiple daily flights

**Hydropower:**
- Nepal's hydropower potential: 83,000 MW
- Joint projects: Pancheshwar, Arun-III
- Power trade agreement
- India's technical and financial support

**Education & Culture:**
- Scholarships for Nepali students
- Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR) programs
- Religious tourism: Mutual facilitation
- Cultural exchanges regular
:::

:::details **Issues in India-Nepal Relations**
**Kalapani Territorial Dispute:**
- Nepal claims Kalapani-Lipulekh-Limpiyadhura area
- India maintains historical position
- Nepal released new political map (2020)
- Issue flared post-India's Mansarovar road inauguration
- Need: Joint survey and historical evidence review

**2015 Constitution & Madhesi Issue:**
- New constitution perceived as discriminatory by Madhesis
- India's concern over inclusivity
- Nepal accused India of "unofficial blockade"
- Strained relations for over a year
- Madhesi protests in Terai region

**Growing Chinese Influence:**
- China's BRI projects in Nepal increasing
- Trans-Himalayan connectivity corridor
- Massive Chinese investments
- Nepal's "balancing act" between India and China
- Security implications for India

**Delayed Projects:**
- Hydropower projects facing delays
- Pricing disputes
- Bureaucratic hurdles
- Funding issues

**Political Instability:**
- Frequent government changes in Nepal
- Communist party's rise and pro-China tilt at times
- Domestic politics affecting India relations
- Coalition governments' unpredictability
:::

:::details **India-Nepal Territorial Dispute - Detailed Analysis**
**Background:**
- Kalapani region: Strategic tri-junction (India-Nepal-China)
- Historically administered by India since 1962 war
- No permanent border posts defined in treaty
- Sugauli Treaty (1816) reference points disputed

**Nepal's Claims:**
- Based on Sugauli Treaty interpretation
- River source demarcation issues
- Maps from British era
- Domestic political pressure to assert sovereignty

**India's Position:**
- Historical administration since independence
- Strategic importance for security
- Boundary Commission work pending completion
- Dialogue-based resolution preferred

**Recent Flashpoint (2019-2020):**
- India's Lipulekh pass road to Mansarovar
- Nepal's constitutional amendment including disputed area
- Diplomatic tensions escalated
- Cooling down through back-channel talks

**Way Forward:**
- Joint technical committee for boundary survey
- Historical records examination
- Diplomatic negotiations without media glare
- Maintain status quo till resolution
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
- **Border Demarcation**: Complete pending survey work
- **Hydropower**: Fast-track stalled projects with clear timelines
- **Madhesi Inclusion**: Encourage inclusive political process
- **Counter Chinese Influence**: Enhanced development assistance
- **Open Border Management**: Better surveillance while maintaining openness
- **Cultural Diplomacy**: Strengthen people-to-people ties
- **Political Engagement**: Regular high-level visits regardless of government
- **Economic Integration**: Facilitate Nepal's trade through Indian ports
:::

## ðŸ‡¦ðŸ‡« INDIA-AFGHANISTAN RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction & Strategic Significance**
**Historical Ties:**
- Ancient civilizational connections through Buddhism
- Gandhara civilization links
- Trade routes: Historic Silk Road
- Cultural exchanges for millennia

**Strategic Importance for India:**
- Gateway to Central Asia
- Counter-Pakistan strategic depth
- Energy corridor: TAPI pipeline potential
- Regional stability crucial for South Asia
- Prevent terror sanctuaries
:::

:::details **Significance of Afghanistan for India**
**Geopolitical:**
- Prevents Pakistan's "strategic depth"
- Access to Central Asian resources
- Chabahar port connectivity
- Balance China-Pakistan axis

**Economic:**
- Afghanistan's mineral wealth: $1-3 trillion
- Trade potential through Iran route
- Market for Indian goods
- Energy corridor possibilities

**Security:**
- Counter terror groups (LeT, JeM presence)
- Prevent radicalization spillover
- Intelligence gathering
- Regional stability imperative
:::

:::details **India-Afghanistan Development Partnership**
**Infrastructure Projects ($3 Billion+ Assistance):**
- **Salma Dam (Afghan-India Friendship Dam)**: 42 MW hydroelectric project
- **Afghan Parliament Building**: Symbol of democracy support
- **Zaranj-Delaram Highway**: 218 km road connecting to Iranian border
- **Shatoot Dam**: Drinking water for Kabul

**Education & Capacity Building:**
- 15,000+ scholarships annually for Afghan students
- Training programs in India for civil servants
- E-learning initiatives
- Technical education support

**Healthcare:**
- Indira Gandhi Children's Hospital, Kabul
- Medical training programs
- Polio vaccination campaigns
- COVID-19 assistance: Vaccines, medicines

**Connectivity:**
- Chabahar Port development (Iran)
- Air freight corridor operational
- Bypass Pakistan route
- Trade facilitation measures

**Agriculture & Rural Development:**
- High-impact community development projects
- Agricultural demonstration programs
- Rural electrification
- Water management projects
:::

:::details **Taliban Takeover of Afghanistan (2021)**
**August 2021 Events:**
- US withdrawal after 20 years
- Rapid Taliban takeover
- Fall of Kabul government
- Evacuation crisis

**India's Response:**
- Closed embassy in Kabul (evacuated staff)
- Operation Devi Shakti: Evacuated Indians and vulnerable Afghans
- Refused to recognize Taliban government initially
- No immediate diplomatic engagement

**Security Concerns:**
- Terror groups regrouping (LeT, JeM, ISKP)
- Taliban-Pakistan nexus
- TTP (Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan) threat
- Al-Qaeda presence
- Loss of Indian intelligence assets

**Development Projects:**
- $3 billion investments at risk
- Projects halted or handed over
- Infrastructure vulnerable
- Uncertainty about Chabahar connectivity
:::

:::details **India's Position Shift on Taliban**
**Initial Stance:**
- Non-recognition of Taliban
- Condemnation of violence
- Support for inclusive government
- Humanitarian focus

**Tactical Engagement:**
- Low-level contacts established
- Focus on humanitarian assistance
- Embassy staff in "technical mission"
- Protecting Indian investments

**Humanitarian Assistance:**
- 50,000 MT wheat supplied
- Medicines and vaccines
- COVID-19 relief
- No cash transfers to Taliban
:::

:::details **Challenges in India-Afghanistan Relations**
**No Direct Land Access:**
- Pakistan blocks transit
- Dependence on air and Chabahar route
- High transportation costs
- Limited trade despite goodwill

**Pakistan-Taliban Nexus:**
- ISI's influence on Taliban
- Safe havens in Pakistan
- Anti-India elements empowered
- Strategic depth concerns

**Terror Threats:**
- Multiple terror groups present
- ISKP (Islamic State Khorasan Province)
- Al-Qaeda in the Indian Subcontinent (AQIS)
- Anti-India Jihadi elements

**Chabahar Port Delays:**
- Iranian sanctions by US
- Slow development
- Competition with Gwadar (Pakistan-China)
- Connectivity to Afghanistan uncertain

**Chinese Footprint:**
- Mining contracts with Taliban
- BRI extension plans
- Economic influence growing
- Geopolitical competition
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
- **Humanitarian Focus**: Continue food, medicine assistance
- **Pragmatic Engagement**: Limited contacts for Indian interests
- **Chabahar Development**: Accelerate despite challenges
- **Regional Coordination**: Work with Central Asian countries
- **SCO Platform**: Use for Afghanistan discussions
- **Cultural Diplomacy**: Support education, heritage preservation
- **Diaspora Leverage**: 30,000 Afghans educated in India
- **Counter-Terror**: Intelligence cooperation with neighbors
- **Long-term View**: Afghanistan situation is fluid, maintain flexibility
:::

:::details **Conclusion**
India-Afghanistan relations face unprecedented challenges post-Taliban takeover. India must balance its strategic interests, humanitarian commitments, and security concerns. While direct engagement with Taliban is limited, India continues humanitarian assistance and maintains tactical contacts. The long-term approach requires patience, pragmatism, and protecting India's significant investments in Afghan goodwill over two decades.
:::
