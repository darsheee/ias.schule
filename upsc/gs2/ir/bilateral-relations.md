# India's Major Bilateral Relations
## USA, Russia, China, EU, Japan, West Asia & More

---

## ðŸ‡ºðŸ‡¸ INDIA-USA RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction & Evolution**
**Historical Context:**
- Cold War: India non-aligned, US allied with Pakistan
- 1971 War: USS Enterprise incident, US supported Pakistan
- 1998 Nuclear tests: US sanctions on India
- Post-2000: Strategic partnership begins

**Current Status:**
- Comprehensive Global Strategic Partnership
- Major Defence Partner (2016)
- 2+2 Dialogue mechanism
- Trade: $191 billion (2022)
:::

:::details **Different Aspects of Indo-US Relations**
**Defence & Security:**
- **Foundational Agreements:**
  - LEMOA (2016): Logistics Exchange Memorandum of Agreement
  - COMCASA (2018): Communications Compatibility and Security Agreement
  - BECA (2020): Basic Exchange and Cooperation Agreement
  - ISA (2023): Industrial Security Annex
- **Joint Exercises:** Malabar (naval), Yudh Abhyas (army), Cope India (air force)
- **Defence Procurement:** $20+ billion worth equipment
- **Technology Transfer:** Jet engines, drones, advanced systems

**Economic Relations:**
- Bilateral trade: $191 billion (2022)
- US largest trading partner
- IT services: Major export to US
- Investment: $54 billion US FDI in India
- Generalized System of Preferences (GSP) withdrawn 2019

**Strategic Cooperation:**
- Indo-Pacific Strategy alignment
- Quad partnership
- Counter-terrorism cooperation
- Intelligence sharing
- Space cooperation
:::

:::details **Energy Cooperation: US-India Strategic Energy Partnership**
**Formation:** 2018, upgraded energy dialogue

**Focus Areas:**
- **LNG Imports**: India major buyer of US LNG
- **Nuclear Energy**: Westinghouse reactors
- **Renewable Energy**: Solar, wind technology
- **Shale Gas**: Technology sharing
- **Energy Security**: Strategic petroleum reserves

**Achievements:**
- US became major oil & gas supplier
- Clean energy technology partnerships
- Joint research on hydrogen, battery storage
- Energy efficiency programs
:::

:::details **India as Counterbalance to China**
**US Perspective:**
- India's rise balances Chinese power
- Democratic partner in Asia
- Indo-Pacific strategy cornerstone
- Alternative to China in supply chains

**India's Approach:**
- Strategic autonomy maintained
- Non-aligned in new form (multi-alignment)
- Issue-based cooperation
- Economic interests balanced

**Cooperation Areas:**
- Quad mechanism
- Infrastructure development (Blue Dot Network)
- Technology partnerships
- Maritime security
:::

:::details **Cooperation at Global Level**
**Multilateral Forums:**
- G20 cooperation
- UN reforms advocacy
- Climate change (Paris Agreement)
- WTO issues

**Counter-Terrorism:**
- Designation of terrorists (Masood Azhar at UN)
- Financial Action Task Force (FATF)
- Intelligence sharing
- Capacity building

**Health Cooperation:**
- Vaccine development partnerships
- Cancer research
- Pandemic preparedness
- Medical education exchanges
:::

:::details **Indo-US Cooperation During Pandemic**
**Vaccine Partnership:**
- Quad Vaccine initiative
- Raw materials supply for Covishield
- Technology sharing
- Distribution coordination

**Medical Supplies:**
- Oxygen concentrators during Delta wave
- Ventilators supply
- PPE kits
- Medicines exchange

**Research Collaboration:**
- Genome sequencing
- Treatment protocols
- Clinical trials
- Public health initiatives
:::

:::details **Issues in Indo-US Relationship**
**Trade Disputes:**
- GSP withdrawal (2019)
- H-1B visa restrictions
- Digital services tax
- Trade deficit ($32 billion in US favor)
- Pharmaceutical price controls
- Data localization requirements

**Defence Challenges:**
- CAATSA (Countering America's Adversaries Through Sanctions Act)
- S-400 purchase from Russia
- Technology transfer restrictions
- End-user monitoring agreements
- Price of military equipment

**Geopolitical Differences:**
- **Russia Relations**: India's continued partnership
- **Iran**: Chabahar vs US sanctions
- **Afghanistan**: Different approaches to Taliban
- **Human Rights**: US concerns over India's record
- **Ukraine War**: India's neutral stance

**Immigration Issues:**
- H-1B visa caps and restrictions
- Green card backlogs for Indians
- Student visa delays
- Healthcare worker visas

**Technology Transfer:**
- Jet engine technology delays
- Semiconductor manufacturing
- Critical technology sharing
- Export controls
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
**Deepen Strategic Partnership:**
- Complete foundational agreements implementation
- Expand defence co-production (DTTI)
- Regular 2+2 dialogues
- High-level visits frequency

**Economic Integration:**
- Resolve trade disputes through dialogue
- Mini trade deal possibilities
- Supply chain resilience partnerships
- Critical minerals cooperation

**Technology Collaboration:**
- iCET (Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technologies)
- Semiconductor partnership
- Space cooperation expansion
- AI and quantum computing

**Indo-Pacific Vision:**
- Infrastructure development (PGII - Partnership for Global Infrastructure Investment)
- Maritime security enhancement
- QUAD deliverables
- Regional stability

**People-to-People:**
- Ease visa restrictions
- Education partnerships
- Cultural exchanges
- Diaspora engagement
:::

:::details **Conclusion**
India-US relations have transformed from Cold War estrangement to comprehensive strategic partnership. While challenges remain, particularly on trade and India's Russia relationship, the convergence on China, terrorism, and Indo-Pacific stability provides strong foundation for cooperation.
:::

## ðŸ‡·ðŸ‡º INDIA-RUSSIA RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction & Historical Overview**
**Cold War Era:**
- 1971 Indo-Soviet Friendship Treaty
- Soviet support during 1971 war (veto at UNSC)
- Defence equipment supplier
- Economic and technological cooperation

**Post-Soviet:**
- 1993 Treaty of Friendship and Cooperation
- 2000 Strategic Partnership declaration
- 2010 Special and Privileged Strategic Partnership
- Annual Summit mechanism (since 2000)
:::

:::details **Areas of Cooperation**
**Defence:**
- **Primary Supplier**: 60-65% of Indian military hardware Russian-origin
- **Major Systems**: Su-30MKI, T-90 tanks, INS Vikramaditya, S-400
- **Joint Production**: BrahMos cruise missile
- **Technology Transfer**: License production in India
- **Nuclear Submarines**: INS Chakra lease

**Nuclear Energy:**
- Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant (6 reactors planned)
- Russian design and technology
- Fuel supply agreements
- Future cooperation in nuclear power

**Space:**
- GLONASS-GPS augmentation
- Gaganyaan mission cooperation
- Satellite launches
- Space technology sharing

**Economic:**
- Trade: $13 billion (2022) - far below potential
- Energy: Oil and gas imports
- Diamonds: Major trade item
- Pharmaceuticals: Indian exports

**Strategic:**
- BRICS partnership
- SCO cooperation
- RIC (Russia-India-China) trilateral
- UN Security Council coordination
:::

:::details **Issues in Relationship**
**Russia-China-Pakistan Axis:**
- Growing Russia-China strategic partnership
- Russia selling weapons to Pakistan
- Joint military exercises (Russia-Pakistan-China)
- SCO dynamics changing
- India's concerns about tilt

**US-Russia Tensions Impact:**
- CAATSA sanctions threat on India
- S-400 deal complications
- Western pressure on India
- India's balancing act difficult
- Technology transfer issues

**Declining Trade:**
- Trade volume stagnant at $13 billion
- Potential of $30 billion unrealized
- Limited economic diversification
- Payment mechanism challenges
- Sanctions impact on trade

**Afghanistan Divergence:**
- Different approaches to Taliban
- Regional security perceptions
- Pakistan factor
- Central Asia influence

**Defence Diversification:**
- India buying from US, France, Israel
- Reducing dependence on Russia
- Make in India push
- Technology absorption needs
:::

:::details **Russia-Ukraine War - Impact on India**
**India's Position:**
- Abstained from UN votes condemning Russia
- Called for dialogue and diplomacy
- Humanitarian assistance to Ukraine
- Oil purchases from Russia increased
- Balanced approach maintaining ties with both

**Concerns for India:**
- Western pressure to condemn Russia
- UNSC permanent member credibility
- Global South leadership
- Defence supplies from Russia
- Energy security (oil imports)

**Opportunities:**
- Cheaper oil from Russia ($80+ billion savings)
- Rupee-Ruble trade mechanism
- Increased bilateral engagement
- Defence cooperation less scrutinized
- Strategic autonomy demonstrated
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
**Diversify Cooperation:**
- Expand beyond defence
- Trade target: $30 billion by 2025
- Technology partnerships
- Energy sector expansion
- Pharmaceuticals and IT

**Strategic Communication:**
- Regular summits continuation
- Track II dialogues
- Military-to-military ties
- Intelligence cooperation
- UN coordination

**Economic Integration:**
- Rupee-Ruble trade mechanism
- Free Trade Agreement with Eurasian Economic Union
- Joint ventures in third countries
- Arctic region cooperation
- Far East development

**Balance Act:**
- Maintain Russia ties despite US pressure
- Manage Russia-China-Pakistan triangle
- Protect defence interests
- Strategic autonomy
- Multi-alignment policy
:::

:::details **Conclusion**
India-Russia relations remain strategically important despite challenges. The "special and privileged" partnership provides India with defence capabilities, energy security, and strategic autonomy. However, changing global dynamics require India to diversify while maintaining this time-tested friendship.
:::

## ðŸ‡¨ðŸ‡³ INDIA-CHINA RELATIONS

:::details **Introduction**
**Historical Context:**
- Ancient civilizational ties (Buddhism)
- Panchsheel Agreement (1954)
- 1962 Border War - watershed moment
- Normalization from 1988
- Trade growth post-2000

**Current Status:**
- Largest trade deficit for India ($101 billion)
- Border tensions ongoing
- Economic interdependence deep
- Strategic competition intense
- LAC standoffs frequent
:::

:::details **India-China Economic Relations**
**Trade Statistics:**
- Bilateral trade: $135.98 billion (2022-23)
- India's exports: $19.8 billion
- India's imports: $101.8 billion
- Trade deficit: $83.2 billion (largest with any country)

**Major Imports from China:**
- Electronic equipment and machinery
- Organic chemicals
- Plastics
- Iron and steel
- Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs)

**Major Exports to China:**
- Ores, slag, ash (iron ore)
- Organic chemicals
- Cotton
- Copper
- Mineral fuels

**Investment:**
- Chinese FDI in India: Restricted post-2020
- Prior approval needed from government
- Concerns over data security
- Apps banned (TikTok, etc.)
:::

:::details **Areas of Relationship: Convergence and Divergence**
**Convergence:**
- BRICS partnership
- SCO cooperation
- Climate change (Basic coalition)
- Multilateral reform (UN, IMF, WTO)
- South-South cooperation
- RIC trilateral

**Divergence:**
- **Border Dispute**: 3488 km LAC unresolved
- **Strategic Competition**: Indo-Pacific vs BRI
- **Pakistan Factor**: China's all-weather friendship
- **Tibet**: Dalai Lama in India
- **Quad**: China opposes India's membership
- **Indian Ocean**: String of Pearls vs SAGAR
- **NSG**: China blocking India's entry
- **UN**: Masood Azhar designation delayed by China
:::

:::details **India-China Cooperation**
**Despite Tensions:**
- BRICS mechanism functional
- Trade continues (with restrictions)
- Academic exchanges (limited)
- Cultural programs
- Border personnel meetings

**Climate Change:**
- Basic countries coalition
- Paris Agreement cooperation
- Clean energy initiatives
- Technology sharing potential

**Multilateral:**
- WTO reform advocacy
- G20 cooperation
- Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB)
- New Development Bank (NDB)
:::

:::details **Possible Factors for Chinese Aggression**
**Strategic:**
- Contain India's rise
- Prevent India-US closeness
- Demonstrate power
- Test Indian resolve
- Domestic nationalism

**Territorial:**
- LAC clarification attempts
- Change status quo
- Infrastructure development
- Military buildup
- Strategic depth

**Economic:**
- BRI criticism by India
- Quad participation
- Alternative supply chains
- Market denial

**Regional:**
- South China Sea parallels
- Salami-slicing tactics
- Grey zone warfare
- Incremental gains
:::

:::details **Border Issues - Detailed**
**Galwan Valley Clash (June 2020):**
- 20 Indian soldiers killed
- Chinese casualties undisclosed
- Worst clash since 1967
- Triggered disengagement process
- Ongoing buffer zones

**Doklam Standoff (2017):**
- 73-day standoff
- Bhutan's territory
- Chinese road construction
- India intervened
- Resolved through diplomacy

**LAC Status:**
- 3488 km long
- Not demarcated
- Different perceptions
- 15 friction points identified
- Disengagement partial
:::

:::details **India-China Water Relations**
**Brahmaputra River:**
- China building dams upstream
- Water flow concerns
- Data sharing agreement (suspended 2020)
- Hydropower projects
- Environmental impact

**Trans-border Rivers:**
- Sutlej, Indus tributaries
- Karnali, Kosi (through Nepal)
- Early warning systems needed
- Climate change impact
- Cooperation framework weak
:::

:::details **China's Rising Influence in South Asia**
**BRI Projects:**
- CPEC in Pakistan ($62 billion)
- Hambantota Port, Sri Lanka
- Maldives: Bridges, housing
- Nepal: Railways, highways
- Bangladesh: Infrastructure

**Debt Trap Diplomacy:**
- Hambantota seized on 99-year lease
- Unsustainable loans
- Strategic assets control
- Economic leverage
- Political influence

**String of Pearls:**
- Gwadar (Pakistan)
- Hambantota (Sri Lanka)
- Kyaukpyu (Myanmar)
- Chittagong (Bangladesh)
- Encircling India

**Impact on India:**
- Strategic encirclement
- Reduced influence
- Security concerns
- Economic competition
- Diplomatic challenges
:::

:::details **India-Taiwan Relations**
**History:**
- No formal diplomatic ties
- Mutual trade offices (not embassies)
- Cultural and economic engagement
- One China Policy observed

**Current State:**
- Trade: $9 billion+
- Semiconductor cooperation important
- IT and electronics partnerships
- People-to-people ties
- Parliamentary exchanges (informal)

**Concerns:**
- China's sensitivity
- Taiwan Strait tensions
- India's balancing act
- Quad dimension
- Economic opportunities vs political risks

**Way Forward:**
- Economic ties deepening
- Technology partnerships (chips)
- Informal engagement
- Avoid provocations
- Strategic ambiguity
:::

:::details **China-Tibet and India**
**Historical:**
- 1950 Chinese occupation
- 1959 Dalai Lama fled to India
- Dharamshala: Tibetan government-in-exile
- 1962 war partly over Tibet

**Current Issues:**
- Dalai Lama succession
- Tibetan refugees in India
- China's pressure on India
- Buddhist diplomacy
- Cultural preservation

**India's Stance:**
- Tibet part of China (officially)
- Humanitarian assistance to refugees
- Dalai Lama spiritual leader, not political
- Cultural and religious freedom support
- Strategic card against China
:::

:::details **Ways Forward**
**Border Management:**
- Complete disengagement at LAC
- Clarify LAC through dialogue
- Confidence-building measures
- Military hotlines effective use
- Prevent escalation protocols

**Economic Rebalancing:**
- Reduce trade deficit
- Diversify supply chains
- Atmanirbhar Bharat implementation
- FDI restrictions on sensitive sectors
- Promote "China+1" strategy

**Strategic Approach:**
- Quad strengthening without provocation
- BRICS engagement continued
- Bilateral dialogue sustained
- Military preparedness enhanced
- Infrastructure development in border areas

**Regional Strategy:**
- Counter BRI with own initiatives
- Deepen South Asia ties
- Act East Policy vigorous implementation
- Indian Ocean presence
- Connectivity alternatives
:::

:::details **Conclusion**
India-China relations are complex, marked by economic interdependence and strategic competition. The 2020 Galwan clash fundamentally altered the relationship. While economic ties persist, trust deficit is deep. India must balance engagement with deterrence, cooperation with competition.
:::
