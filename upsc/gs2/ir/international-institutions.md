## üèõÔ∏è UNITED NATIONS & INDIA

:::details **Introduction to UN**
**Formation:**
- Established: October 24, 1945
- Headquarters: New York, USA
- Members: 193 countries
- Founders: 51 countries (India was founding member)

**Principal Organs:**
1. General Assembly
2. Security Council
3. Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)
4. Trusteeship Council
5. International Court of Justice
6. Secretariat
:::

:::details **Issues in United Nations**
**UNSC Reform:**
- Outdated structure (1945 power balance)
- Veto power of P5 (US, UK, France, Russia, China)
- Lack of representation for Africa, Latin America, Asia
- India, Japan, Germany, Brazil (G4) seeking permanent seats
- Africa seeking 2 permanent seats

**Funding Crisis:**
- US withholds contributions
- Peacekeeping budget constraints
- Administrative inefficiency
- Over-reliance on few donors

**Effectiveness:**
- Syria, Yemen, Ukraine - unable to act
- P5 veto paralyzes action
- Peacekeeping operations underresourced
- Human rights violations inadequately addressed

**Emerging Challenges:**
- Terrorism not adequately addressed
- Climate change coordination weak
- Cyber warfare ungoverned
- Artificial Intelligence regulation needed
- Pandemics response inadequate
:::

:::details **Reforms Needed**
**Security Council:**
- Expand permanent members (include G4 + Africa)
- Veto reform (restrict or eliminate)
- Regional representation
- Regular review of membership

**General Assembly:**
- Revitalization needed
- Effective follow-up mechanisms
- Reduce overlap with ECOSOC
- More accountability

**Secretariat:**
- Administrative efficiency
- Transparency in appointments
- Performance metrics
- Cost reduction

**Peacekeeping:**
- Adequate funding
- Clear mandates
- Better equipment
- Exit strategies
:::

:::details **India and UN ECOSOC**
**ECOSOC Role:**
- 54 members (18 elected annually for 3-year terms)
- Coordinates economic, social, humanitarian work
- 70% of UN system resources and personnel

**India's Engagement:**
- Regular member since independence
- Elected multiple times
- Active in subsidiary bodies
- Development agenda advocate

**Recent (2022):**
- Elected to 4 UN ECOSOC bodies:
  - Commission on Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice
  - Commission on Population and Development
  - Commission on Science and Technology for Development
  - Executive Board of UN Entity for Gender Equality
:::

:::details **India's Issues with UN Organizations**
**Counter-Terrorism:**
- Comprehensive Convention on International Terrorism pending since 1996
- Pakistan's blocking tactics
- Masood Azhar designation delayed by China
- Definitions disputed

**Human Rights Council:**
- Selective approach criticized
- Politicization of issues
- India's record questioned (Kashmir, minorities)
- Double standards perceived

**WHO:**
- COVID-19 origin investigation inadequate
- China's influence
- Traditional medicine recognition delayed
- Transparency issues

**UNSC:**
- No permanent seat despite contributions
- 2nd largest troop contributor to peacekeeping
- Financial contributions significant
- Asia underrepresented
:::

:::details **India's Key Challenges at UN**
**Permanent Membership:**
- China's opposition
- Coffee Club resistance
- Africa's competing claims
- Reform consensus difficult

**Pakistan Factor:**
- Kashmir internationalization attempts
- OIC support for Pakistan
- Terrorism sponsorship unpunished
- Cross-border issues

**Western Pressure:**
- Human rights record scrutiny
- Democratic backsliding allegations
- Minority rights concerns
- CAA-NRC international criticism

**Climate Finance:**
- Developed countries' commitments unfulfilled
- $100 billion target missed
- Technology transfer inadequate
- Common But Differentiated Responsibilities (CBDR) dilution

**Representation:**
- Indian nationals in senior UN positions low
- Bureaucratic challenges
- Competitive exams needed
:::

:::details **United Nations Security Council (UNSC)**
**Structure:**
- **Permanent Members (P5)**: US, UK, France, Russia, China (veto power)
- **Non-Permanent Members**: 10 countries (2-year terms)
- **Functions**: Peace and security, sanctions, military action authorization

**India's UNSC Journey:**
- Non-permanent member 8 times (most recent: 2021-22)
- Permanent seat aspirant
- G4 coalition (India, Japan, Germany, Brazil)
- "United for Consensus" group opposes expansion

**India's Contributions:**
- **Peacekeeping**: 2nd largest troop contributor (cumulative)
- **Financial**: Among top 10 contributors
- **Terrorism**: Counter-terrorism efforts
- **Development**: South-South cooperation
- **Climate**: Renewable energy leadership

**Obstacles to Permanent Seat:**
- China's opposition (geopolitical rivalry)
- Pakistan lobbying against
- Coffee Club (Italy, Pakistan, others)
- P5 reluctance to dilute power
- Africa's insistence on its representation first
:::

:::details **Way Forward for UN Reform**
**Short Term:**
- UNSC working methods reform
- Transparency in veto use
- Regular P5 accountability
- Strengthen General Assembly

**Medium Term:**
- Expand UNSC to 25-26 members
- Include G4 + 2 African countries
- Veto restriction on humanitarian issues
- Better regional representation

**Long Term:**
- Eliminate veto power
- Democratic UNSC election
- Strengthen International Court of Justice
- Create new bodies for emerging challenges (cyber, AI, space)

**India's Strategy:**
- Continue peacekeeping contributions
- Lead on climate, SDGs
- Build coalitions (G4, G77, NAM)
- Increase financial contributions
- Showcase democratic credentials
:::

## üè• WORLD HEALTH ORGANIZATION (WHO) & INDIA

:::details **Introduction to WHO**
**Formation:**
- Established: April 7, 1948
- Headquarters: Geneva, Switzerland
- Members: 194 countries
- Budget: ~$6 billion (2022-23 biennium)

**Structure:**
- World Health Assembly (decision-making body)
- Executive Board (34 members)
- Secretariat (implementation)
- 6 Regional Offices

**Mandate and Functions:**
- Coordinate global health responses
- Set norms and standards
- Provide technical assistance
- Monitor health trends
- Research and data collection
:::

:::details **India and WHO**
**Membership:**
- Founding member (1948)
- Active in South-East Asia Regional Office
- Regular Executive Board member
- Significant financial contributions

**Four Strategic Priorities of WHO in India:**
1. **Universal Health Coverage**: Ayushman Bharat support
2. **Health Emergencies**: Pandemic preparedness
3. **Healthier Populations**: NCD prevention, maternal-child health
4. **Organizational Excellence**: Data systems, capacity building

**WHO India Country Cooperation Strategy 2019-2023:**
- Focus on SDG 3 (Health for All)
- Support government programs
- Technical assistance
- Capacity building
- Emergency preparedness
:::

:::details **India-WHO Collaboration Highlights**
**COVID-19 Response:**
- Technical guidance to India
- Vaccine approval (Covaxin, Covishield)
- Surveillance support
- Training programs
- Equipment supply

**Disease Eradication:**
- Polio: India declared polio-free (2014)
- Tuberculosis: End TB Strategy
- Malaria: Elimination by 2030 target
- Kala-azar elimination achieved

**Traditional Medicine:**
- WHO Traditional Medicine Global Centre in Jamnagar, Gujarat (2022)
- AYUSH integration
- Evidence-based traditional medicine
- Global knowledge hub

**Pandemic Preparedness:**
- Disease surveillance systems
- Laboratory network strengthening
- Rapid response teams
- International Health Regulations (IHR) compliance
:::

:::details **Criticism of WHO**
**COVID-19 Handling:**
- Delayed pandemic declaration (January 2020 concerns, declared March 2020)
- Initial downplaying of human-to-human transmission
- Travel restrictions advice conflicting
- Origin investigation inadequate
- China's influence alleged

**China Factor:**
- Praised China's response despite cover-ups
- Wuhan lab leak theory dismissed prematurely
- Taiwan excluded (China's pressure)
- Transparency lacking

**Funding Issues:**
- Over-dependence on Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation
- US withdrawal threat (later rejoined)
- Voluntary contributions exceed assessed
- Donor priorities influence agenda

**Structural Problems:**
- Slow decision-making
- Bureaucratic inefficiency
- Political interference
- Inadequate enforcement powers
- Rich country bias
:::

:::details **Recent Developments**
**Pandemic Treaty:**
- Negotiations ongoing for legally binding instrument
- India's concerns: Equity, IPR, technology transfer
- Deadline extended to 2024
- Sovereignty vs. global health balance

**Traditional Medicine Centre (Jamnagar):**
- First WHO global centre for traditional medicine
- $250 million investment
- AYUSH recognition milestone
- Research and evidence generation

**WHO Emergency Use Listing:**
- Covaxin approved (November 2021)
- Boosted India's vaccine diplomacy
- Facilitated international travel
- Validated Indian vaccine prowess

**mRNA Hub:**
- WHO establishing in India
- Vaccine self-reliance
- Technology transfer
- Pandemic preparedness
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
**Reform WHO:**
- Independent funding mechanism
- Reduce donor influence
- Strengthen enforcement powers
- Faster decision-making

**India's Role:**
- Increase assessed contributions
- Leadership in Executive Board
- Promote traditional medicine globally
- Pandemic preparedness hub for region

**Pandemic Treaty:**
- Protect sovereignty
- Ensure equity in vaccine access
- Technology transfer mechanisms
- TRIPS waiver advocacy

**Global Health Leadership:**
- Vaccine production capacity
- Generic medicines supply
- Medical tourism destination
- Health diplomacy
:::

## üåç WORLD TRADE ORGANIZATION (WTO) & INDIA

:::details **Introduction to WTO**
**Formation:**
- Established: January 1, 1995 (replaced GATT)
- Headquarters: Geneva, Switzerland
- Members: 164 countries
- Budget: CHF 197 million (2023)

**Functions:**
1. Administer trade agreements
2. Forum for trade negotiations
3. Handle trade disputes
4. Monitor national trade policies
5. Technical assistance to developing countries
6. Cooperate with other international organizations

**Governance:**
- Ministerial Conference (highest body, meets every 2 years)
- General Council (day-to-day work)
- Dispute Settlement Body
- Trade Policy Review Body
:::

:::details **Achievement of WTO**
**Trade Liberalization:**
- Reduced average tariffs globally
- Eliminated many non-tariff barriers
- Increased global trade volumes
- Multilateral trading system

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Binding dispute settlement mechanism
- 600+ cases adjudicated
- Rules-based system
- Alternative to trade wars

**Developing Countries:**
- Special and Differential Treatment
- Technical assistance
- Capacity building
- Longer implementation periods
:::

:::details **Issues Related to WTO**
**Doha Round Stalemate:**
- Launched 2001, still incomplete
- Agriculture subsidies by developed countries
- Developing vs. developed divide
- Services liberalization disputes

**Appellate Body Crisis:**
- US blocking appointments since 2019
- Dispute resolution paralyzed
- Only 1 member remaining (need 3 for quorum)
- Undermines WTO's core function

**E-Commerce:**
- No rules on digital trade
- Data localization debates
- Taxation of digital services
- IPR in digital space

**Subsidy Disputes:**
- Fisheries subsidies
- Agricultural subsidies by US, EU
- Developing countries' policy space
- Industrial subsidies

**Plurilateral Agreements:**
- Some members-only deals
- Undermines multilateralism
- Two-tier system emerging
- Free riders problem
:::

:::details **WTO and India**
**Membership:**
- Founding member (1995)
- Active in all councils
- Lead coordinator for G33 (developing countries)
- Defender of multilateralism

**Trade Interests:**
- Agriculture: Protect farmers from import surges
- Services: Mode 4 (movement of professionals) liberalization
- Intellectual Property: TRIPS flexibilities
- Special and Differential Treatment

**India's Demands:**
- Public stockholding for food security (permanent solution)
- Developed countries reduce farm subsidies
- Mode 4 of GATT (professionals' movement)
- Technology transfer

**India's Concerns:**
- Developed countries' high agricultural subsidies
- Tariff peaks on developing country exports
- IPR rules favor pharmaceutical companies
- Services liberalization pressures
:::

:::details **Reason for Breaching Subsidy Ceiling**
**Issue:**
- India's food procurement exceeds 10% of agricultural production value (WTO limit)
- Rice, wheat procurement for Public Distribution System (PDS)
- Calculated on 1986-88 prices (artificially low base)

**India's Stand:**
- Food security non-negotiable
- Developing country right
- Temporary measure until permanent solution
- Peace Clause protection

**Status:**
- Permanent solution still pending
- Bali Package (2013) gave interim relief
- Developed countries reluctant
:::

:::details **7th Trade Policy Review of India (2021) Highlights**
**WTO Assessment:**
- India's trade policy generally open
- Tariffs increased in some sectors (protectionism concern)
- Regulatory environment improving
- Services sector reforms positive

**India's Response:**
- Atmanirbhar Bharat explained
- Tariff increases within WTO limits
- Protective measures temporary
- Ease of Doing Business improvements highlighted

**Issues Raised:**
- Import restrictions on IT products
- Data localization requirements
- Agricultural market access
- Subsidy transparency
:::

:::details **India Challenges WTO Panel Ruling on ICT Import Duties**
**Background:**
- 2016: EU, Japan, Taiwan challenged India's tariffs on ICT products
- Information Technology Agreement (ITA) compliance disputed
- India argued products not covered under ITA
- 2019: Panel ruled against India

**India's Appeal:**
- Filed at Appellate Body (2019)
- Appealed to non-functioning AB ("appeal into void")
- Protects India's right to appeal
- Issue remains unresolved due to AB crisis

**Implications:**
- Import duties continue
- Domestic manufacturing protected
- Make in India for electronics
- Precedent for other developing countries
:::

:::details **12th Ministerial Conference of WTO (Geneva 2022)**
**Key Outcomes:**
- **Fisheries Subsidies Agreement**: Prohibit subsidies for illegal, unreported, unregulated (IUU) fishing
- **TRIPS Waiver**: Limited waiver for COVID-19 vaccines
- **Food Security**: Work program established (no permanent solution)
- **E-Commerce**: Moratorium on customs duties extended
- **WTO Reform**: Commitment to revitalize, including AB

**India's Wins:**
- TRIPS waiver (though limited)
- Food security work program
- Fisheries: Special and Differential Treatment for developing countries
- Public stockholding interim protection

**India's Concerns:**
- TRIPS waiver limited (vaccines only, not diagnostics/therapeutics)
- Permanent solution on food security pending
- Developed countries' subsidies continue
- E-commerce moratorium may hurt digital taxation
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
**WTO Reform:**
- Restore Appellate Body functioning
- Modernize rules for digital economy
- Address subsidy distortions
- Strengthen developing country voice

**India's Strategy:**
- Coalition building (G33, G20)
- Defend food security policy space
- Push for Mode 4 liberalization
- Protect domestic industries strategically
- Engage in plurilaterals selectively

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Use Multi-Party Interim Appeal Arbitration Arrangement (MPIA)
- Bilateral settlements where possible
- Strengthen domestic legal capacity
- Proactive compliance

**Negotiations:**
- Permanent solution on public stockholding
- Fisheries subsidies implementation with S&DT
- E-commerce: Protect policy space
- Investment facilitation: Careful engagement
:::
