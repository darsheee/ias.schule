## Non-State Actors in Democracy

## üéØ Types of Pressure Groups

:::details **Institutional Groups**
- **Business Associations**: FICCI, CII, ASSOCHAM
  - Influence economic policies
  - Lobby for favorable regulations
  - Represent corporate interests
- **Trade Unions**: INTUC, AITUC, BMS, CITU
  - Worker rights and welfare
  - Collective bargaining
  - Political affiliations
- **Professional Bodies**: IMA, Bar Council, CA Institute
  - Professional standards
  - Regulatory influence
  - Ethical codes
:::

:::details **Non-Associational Groups**
- **Caste Groups**: Caste associations and sanghatans
  - Political mobilization
  - Reservation demands
  - Social status claims
- **Religious Groups**: 
  - Denominational organizations
  - Religious reform movements
  - Political influence on communal issues
- **Linguistic Groups**:
  - Language protection movements
  - State reorganization demands
  - Cultural preservation
:::

:::details **Associational Groups (NGOs/CSOs)**
- **Development NGOs**: Focus on welfare and service delivery
- **Advocacy Groups**: Policy change and rights-based work
- **Environmental Groups**: Greenpeace, WWF, CSE
- **Human Rights Groups**: PUCL, Amnesty, HRW
- **Women's Groups**: SEWA, AIDWA, feminist organizations
- **Think Tanks**: CPR, ICRIER, ORF
:::

:::details **Anomic Groups**
- **Spontaneous Groups**: Protest movements
- **Crisis-Based**: Form during specific events
- **Farmer Protests**: MSP demands, loan waivers
- **Student Movements**: Fee hikes, policy changes
- **Anti-CAA Protests**: Citizenship law opposition
- **Jallikattu Protests**: Cultural rights assertion

## ‚öñÔ∏è Regulation of NGOs & Civil Society

:::details **Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 2010 (amended 2020)**
- **FCRA Registration**: Mandatory for receiving foreign funds
- **Purpose**: Regulate foreign donations for national interest
- **2020 Amendments**:
  - Aadhaar mandatory for registration
  - 20% cap on administrative expenses
  - Government servant definition expanded
  - Surrender of certificate before expiry not allowed
  - Sub-granting prohibited
- **Criticism**: 
  - Restricts legitimate civil society work
  - Vague "public interest" definition
  - Discretionary powers to government
:::

:::details **Income Tax Act & Tax Exemptions**
- **Section 12A/80G**: Tax exemptions for charitable organizations
- **Registration Requirements**: Detailed documentation
- **Annual Returns**: Form 10B, audit reports
- **Cancellation**: If activities not genuine
- **Compliance Burden**: Particularly for small NGOs
:::

:::details **Other Regulatory Frameworks**
- **Societies Registration Act, 1860**: Register societies
- **Trust Act**: State-specific trust laws
- **Section 8 Companies Act**: Not-for-profit companies
- **Home Ministry Oversight**: Prior permission for foreign visits
- **Intelligence Bureau Clearance**: For FCRA
- **State-Level Regulations**: Additional compliance in some states
:::

:::details **Recent Actions & Controversies**
- **FCRA Licenses**: Thousands cancelled/not renewed
  - Amnesty International India
  - Lawyers Collective
  - Sabrang Trust
  - Over 6000 NGOs between 2011-2021
- **Sedition Charges**: Against activists and NGOs
- **Foreign Funding Allegations**: National security concerns cited
- **Bank Account Freezing**: Sudden and without notice
- **Civil Society Crackdown**: International criticism
:::

## üìä Challenges & Concerns

:::details **Government-Civil Society Relations**
- **Adversarial Approach**: Growing mistrust
- **Space Shrinking**: Regulatory restrictions increasing
- **Vilification**: "Anti-national" labeling
- **Intimidation**: Raids, arrests of activists
- **Defunding**: FCRA cancellations cripple operations
- **Co-option**: Only "friendly" NGOs get space
:::

:::details **Accountability & Transparency**
- **Financial Opacity**: Some NGOs lack transparency
- **Fake NGOs**: Shell organizations for money laundering
- **Performance Metrics**: Difficult to measure impact
- **Governance Issues**: Internal democracy lacking
- **Founder Syndrome**: Over-centralized control
- **Audit Compliance**: Weak in many small NGOs
:::

:::details **Sustainability & Funding**
- **Donor Dependency**: Unsustainable funding models
- **Project Mode**: Short-term thinking
- **Overhead Restrictions**: 20% cap inadequate
- **Declining CSR**: Corporate social responsibility reducing
- **Foreign Funding**: FCRA restrictions impact
- **Local Fundraising**: Underdeveloped in India
:::

:::details **Representativeness Questions**
- **Elite Dominance**: Urban, English-speaking activists
- **Lack of Mass Base**: Disconnected from communities
- **Self-Appointed**: No electoral mandate
- **Agenda Setting**: External donor priorities
- **Urban Bias**: Rural issues neglected
- **Class Character**: Middle-class leadership
:::

## üåü Successful Civil Society Movements

:::details **Right to Information Movement**
- **MKSS**: Mazdoor Kisan Shakti Sangathan in Rajasthan
- **Jan Sunwais**: Public hearings on corruption
- **RTI Act 2005**: National law enacted
- **Impact**: Transformed governance and accountability
- **Replicable Model**: Spread to other states and issues
:::

:::details **Anti-Corruption Movement**
- **India Against Corruption (2011)**: Anna Hazare-led
- **Lokpal Demand**: Ombudsman for corruption
- **Mass Mobilization**: Urban middle-class participation
- **Lokpal Act 2013**: After sustained pressure
- **Limitations**: Weakened in final form
- **Legacy**: Energized political engagement
:::

:::details **Environmental Movements**
- **Chipko Movement**: Forest conservation in Uttarakhand
- **Narmada Bachao Andolan**: Against mega-dams
- **Silent Valley**: Saved from hydroelectric project
- **Western Ghats Protection**: Gadgil Committee
- **Anti-Plastic Campaigns**: Behavior change success
- **Climate Activism**: Growing youth participation
:::

:::details **Women's Rights Movements**
- **SEWA**: Self-employed women's economic empowerment
- **Nirbhaya Protests (2012)**: Criminal law amendments
- **#MeToo Movement**: Workplace harassment awareness
- **Pink Chaddi Campaign**: Against moral policing
- **One Billion Rising**: Violence against women
- **Legal Reforms**: Progressive laws enacted
:::

:::details **Farmers' Movements**
- **Farm Laws Protests (2020-21)**: Largest in recent times
  - MSP guarantee demand
  - Corporate agriculture opposition
  - Three farm laws repealed
- **Kisan Mukti March**: Debt relief demands
- **Land Acquisition Protests**: Against SEZs, projects
- **Impact**: Policy reversals, political consequences
:::
