# Indian Judiciary
## Structure, Independence & Judicial Review

---

## üèõÔ∏è Judicial Structure

:::details **Three-Tier System**
- **Supreme Court**: Apex court
- **High Courts**: State level (25)
- **Subordinate Courts**: District, session
- **Tribunals**: Specialized adjudication
:::

:::details **Supreme Court**
- **Establishment**: 26 Jan 1950
- **Judges**: 1 CJI +  33 others
- **Jurisdiction**: Original, Appellate, Advisory
- **Article 32**: Writ jurisdiction
- **Retirement**: 65 years age
:::

:::details **High Courts**
- **Article 214**: Establishment
- **25 High Courts**: Currently
- **Judges**: Vary state-wise
- **Writ Jurisdiction**: Article 226
- **Retirement**: 62 years age
:::

:::details **Subordinate Courts**
- **District Judges**: Civil matters
- **Sessions Judges**: Criminal matters
- **Magistrates**: Lower criminal
- **Munsiffs**: Lower civil
- **State Judiciary**: Under state control
:::

## ‚öñÔ∏è Judicial Independence

:::details **Constitutional Safeguards**
- **Security of Tenure**: Fixed term
- **Salary**: Charged on Consolidated Fund
- **Removal**: Difficult process impeachment
- **Powers**: Cannot be curtailed
- **Separation**: From executive
:::

:::details **Appointment Process**
- **Collegium System**: 1993 established
- **CJI**: 4 senior judges
- **Recommendations**: Binding on government
- **Primacy**: Judiciary
- **Transparency**: Lack criticized
:::

:::details **Threats to Independence**
- **Executive Interference**: Appointments
- **Financial Constraints**: Inadequate budgets
- **Media Trial**: Public pressure
- **Pendency**: Case backlog
- **Political Influence**: Alleged
:::
