# Terrorism
## Cross-Border, Radicalization, and Counter-Terrorism

---

## VARIANTS OF TERRORISM

:::details **Classification by Second ARC**
**1. Cross-Border Terrorism:**
- Sponsored by Pakistan (LeT, JeM)
- Infiltration through LoC
- Training camps in PoK

**2. Jammu & Kashmir Terrorism:**
- Separatist movements
- Valley-based militancy
- Hybrid terrorists

**3. Northeast Insurgency:**
- Ethnic-based terrorism
- Demand for autonomy/independence
- External support from China, Myanmar

**4. Left-Wing Extremism:**
- Maoist ideology
- Red Corridor states
- Anti-state violence

**5. Communal Terrorism:**
- Religious fundamentalism
- Riots and targeted violence
- Social media-driven radicalization
:::

## PAK-SPONSORED / CROSS-BORDER TERRORISM

:::details **Major Terror Groups**
**Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT):**
- Founded: 1990
- Founder: Hafiz Saeed
- Major Attacks: 2008 Mumbai attacks, 2001 Parliament attack
- Focus: Kashmir, anti-India operations

**Jaish-e-Mohammad (JeM):**
- Founded: 2000
- Founder: Masood Azhar
- Major Attacks: 2019 Pulwama, 2001 Parliament, 2016 Pathankot
- Suicide bombing expertise

**Hizbul Mujahideen (HM):**
- Indigenous Kashmiri group
- Founded: 1989
- Leader: Syed Salahuddin (designated global terrorist by US)

**Al-Badr:**
- Pakistan-based
- Active in Kashmir valley
- Affiliated with LeT

**Harkat-ul-Mujahideen:**
- Founded: 1985
- Links with Al-Qaeda
- Taliban connections
:::

:::details **Factors Facilitating Cross-Border Incursion**
**Geographical:**
- 3,323 km India-Pakistan border
- Difficult terrain (mountains, forests)
- Porous border sections
- Riverine areas

**Pakistani Support:**
- ISI active support
- Training infrastructure in PoK
- Arms and ammunition supply
- Financial support through hawala

**Local Facilitators:**
- Overground Workers (OGWs)
- Local support network
- Safe houses
- Intelligence gathering

**Technology Misuse:**
- Encrypted communications
- Dark web recruitment
- Drones for arms dropping
- Underground tunnels

**International Linkages:**
- Gulf-based funding
- Diaspora support
- Narco-terrorism revenue
- Hawala networks
:::

:::details **India's Counter-Terrorism Response**
**Military Operations:**
- **2016 Surgical Strikes**: Special forces operations across LoC
- **2019 Balakot Air Strike**: Pre-emptive strike on JeM camp
- **Operation All Out**: Ongoing anti-terror operations
- **Cordon and Search Operations (CASO)**

**Border Management:**
- Smart fencing with sensors
- BOLD-QIT (Border Electronically Dominated QRT Interception Technique)
- Comprehensive Integrated Border Management System (CIBMS)
- Thermal imaging and night vision devices
- Increased BSF strength

**Intelligence Coordination:**
- Multi-Agency Centre (MAC)
- Subsidiary Multi-Agency Centre (SMAC) in states
- Joint Intelligence Committee (JIC)
- National Technical Research Organisation (NTRO)

**International Diplomacy:**
- FATF grey-listing of Pakistan
- UN sanctions on terrorists (Masood Azhar, Hafiz Saeed)
- Bilateral pressure on Pakistan
- Isolating Pakistan internationally
:::

## RADICALIZATION

:::details **Concept and Process**
**Definition:**
- Process of adopting extremist ideology
- Willingness to use violence for political/ideological goals
- Rejection of democratic processes

**Radicalization Pathway:**
1. **Pre-radicalization**: Vulnerable individuals
2. **Self-identification**: Exposure to extremist ideology
3. **Indoctrination**: Accepting extremist worldview
4. **Jihadization**: Actual involvement in terror activities

**Push Factors:**
- Socio-economic marginalization
- Discrimination (real or perceived)
- Political alienation
- Identity crisis
- Revenge for perceived injustices

**Pull Factors:**
- Ideology attraction
- Sense of belonging
- Peer influence
- Charismatic leadership
- Reward promises (martyrdom, paradise)

**Modern Radicalization:**
- **Internet-based**: Social media, encrypted apps
- **Self-radicalization**: Without physical contact
- **Foreign Fighters**: ISIS recruitment from India
- **Returnees**: Trained fighters coming back
:::

:::details **ISIS and Threats to India**
**ISIS Presence in India:**
- **Limited but Growing**: 100-200 individuals influenced
- **Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra**: Maximum cases
- **Social Media Recruitment**: Telegram, encrypted apps
- **Lone Wolf Attacks**: Radicalized individuals acting alone

**Major Cases:**
- **2016**: ISIS module busted in UP (plotted attacks)
- **Kannaur, Kerala**: 21 individuals left for Afghanistan/Syria (2016)
- **2019**: Easter Sunday Bombings in Sri Lanka (India connection)
- **NIA Investigations**: Multiple modules across India

**Why India Not Major Target:**
- Strong intelligence network
- Muslim community largely moderate
- Diverse Islamic traditions (Sufi, Barelvi)
- Economic opportunities
- Democratic channels for grievances

**Threats:**
- Returning fighters from Syria/Afghanistan
- Radicalization through online propaganda
- Lone wolf attacks possibility
- Soft targets (crowded places)
:::

:::details **De-radicalization and Counter-Radicalization**
**De-radicalization:**
- Process of disengaging from extremist ideology
- Rehabilitation of radicalized individuals
- Psychological counseling
- Vocational training
- Family and community reintegration

**Counter-Radicalization:**
- **Preventive Measures**: Before radicalization occurs
- **Community Engagement**: Involving local communities
- **Counter-Narrative**: Challenging extremist propaganda
- **Education**: Critical thinking, democratic values
- **Opportunity Creation**: Employment, education

**Best Practices Globally:**
- **Singapore**: Religious Rehabilitation Group (RRG)
- **Saudi Arabia**: Muhammad bin Nayef Counseling Center
- **UK**: Prevent Strategy
- **France**: Deradicalization centers
- **Indonesia**: Desistance programs

**India's Approach:**
- **Counseling Programs**: For arrested youth
- **Family Counseling**: Engaging families
- **Community Leaders**: Religious scholars involvement
- **Educational Programs**: In vulnerable areas
- **Skill Development**: Livelihood opportunities

**Challenges:**
- No dedicated national program
- Ad-hoc state-level initiatives
- Lack of trained counselors
- Stigmatization of beneficiaries
- Monitoring post-release

**Way Forward:**
- National de-radicalization policy
- Training for counselors and psychologists
- Community-based approach
- Focus on prevention
- Counter-narrative development
- Technology for monitoring extremist content
:::

## INSTITUTIONAL FRAMEWORK

:::details **Counter-Terrorism Agencies**
**National Investigation Agency (NIA):**
- **Established**: 2009 (post-26/11)
- **Mandate**: Investigate terror-related offences
- **Pan-India Jurisdiction**: Can investigate anywhere
- **Special Powers**: Under NIA Act 2008
- **Recent Amendments**: UAPA Amendment Act 2019 empowered NIA

**Intelligence Bureau (IB):**
- **Role**: Internal intelligence
- **Counter-terrorism**: Primary responsibility
- **Multi-Agency Centre (MAC)**: Coordination hub
- **State Subsidiary MACs**: At state level

**Research and Analysis Wing (RAW):**
- **External Intelligence**: Focus on Pakistan, China
- **Counter-terrorism**: External dimensions
- **Covert Operations**: Special operations division

**National Security Council (NSC):**
- **Advisory Body**: To Prime Minister
- **National Security Advisor**: Ajit Doval
- **Strategic Policy Centre (SPC)**
- **National Security Advisory Board (NSAB)**

**Anti-Terrorism Squad (ATS):**
- **State-Level**: Each state has ATS
- **Investigation**: Terror cases
- **Coordination**: With central agencies

**National Technical Research Organisation (NTRO):**
- **Technical Intelligence**: SIGINT, IMINT
- **Surveillance**: Electronic surveillance
- **Satellite Imagery**: Analysis

**Defence Intelligence Agency (DIA):**
- **Military Intelligence**: Coordination
- **Threat Assessment**: External and internal
- **Support to Armed Forces**
:::

:::details **Legislative Measures**
**Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act (UAPA), 1967:**
- **Banning Organizations**: Power to declare unlawful
- **Designated Terrorists**: Individuals can be designated (2019 Amendment)
- **Terrorism Definition**: Acts intended to threaten unity, integrity, security
- **Enhanced Punishment**: Death penalty for certain offences
- **Investigation Period**: Extended to 180 days
- **Attachment of Property**: Of designated individuals/organizations

**NIA Act, 2008:**
- **Special Agency**: For terror investigation
- **Suo Motu Powers**: Can take over cases
- **Special Courts**: Designated NIA courts
- **Pan-India Jurisdiction**: Anywhere in India

**Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA), 2002:**
- **Terror Financing**: Covered as predicate offence
- **Proceeds of Crime**: Attachment and confiscation
- **Enforcement Directorate**: Investigation agency
- **International Cooperation**: FATF compliance

**The Suppression of Unlawful Acts against Safety of Civil Aviation Act, 1982:**
- **Aircraft Hijacking**: Prevention and punishment
- **ICAO Compliance**: International convention implementation

**Anti-Hijacking Act, 2016:**
- **Death Penalty**: For hijacking resulting in death
- **Life Imprisonment**: Other hijacking cases
- **Jurisdiction**: Extended to foreign aircraft

**Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act (TADA):**
- **Period**: 1985-1995 (lapsed)
- **Controversial**: Misuse allegations
- **Not Renewed**: Human rights concerns

**Prevention of Terrorism Act (POTA), 2002:**
- **Period**: 2002-2004 (repealed)
- **Stringent Provisions**: Confessions admissible
- **Misuse**: Against minorities, political opponents
- **Repealed**: 2004
:::

:::details **Issues with Anti-Terror Legislation**
**Human Rights Concerns:**
- Arbitrary arrests and detention
- Custodial torture
- Confession under coercion
- Denial of bail
- Prolonged trials

**Misuse:**
- Against political opponents
- Targeting minorities
- Silencing dissent
- False cases

**Procedural Issues:**
- Burden of proof reversal (accused must prove innocence)
- Confessions to police admissible (under POTA, now repealed)
- Extended detention without trial
- Stringent bail provisions

**Balance Required:**
- Security vs civil liberties
- Effective law enforcement vs human rights
- Swift justice vs fair trial

**Way Forward:**
- Judicial oversight strengthened
- Sunset clauses in laws
- Independent monitoring
- Training for investigators
- Protection against misuse
- Compensation for wrongful prosecution
:::

## EMERGING CHALLENGES

:::details **Lone Wolf Attacks**
**Concept:**
- Individual acting alone
- Self-radicalized through internet
- No direct organizational link
- Difficult to detect

**Characteristics:**
- Minimal planning
- Soft targets (crowded places)
- Low-cost operations
- Maximum publicity

**Examples Globally:**
- **2016 Nice Attack**: Truck ramming, 86 killed
- **2019 Christchurch**: Mosque shootings, 51 killed
- **2017 Manchester**: Concert bombing, 22 killed
- **2016 Orlando**: Nightclub shooting, 49 killed

**India's Vulnerability:**
- Large population concentrations
- Festivals and gatherings
- Soft targets abundant
- Limited surveillance

**Counter-Measures:**
- **Community Policing**: Local intelligence
- **Social Media Monitoring**: Track radicalization
- **Behavioral Analysis**: Identify red flags
- **Public Awareness**: Reporting suspicious activities
- **Target Hardening**: Security at crowded places
:::

:::details **Hybrid Terrorists**
**Definition:**
- Civilians with legitimate jobs
- Provide logistical support to terrorists
- Not full-time militants
- Act as overground workers

**Role:**
- **Intelligence Gathering**: On security forces
- **Logistics Support**: Shelter, food, transport
- **Recruitment**: Identifying potential recruits
- **Stone Pelting**: During encounters
- **Propaganda**: Social media activities

**Challenges:**
- Difficult to identify
- Legal protection as civilians
- Blend in local population
- Can resume normal life quickly

**J&K Context:**
- Increasing phenomenon post-2016
- Youth involved in stone pelting
- Part-time terror activities
- Return to studies/jobs

**Counter-Strategy:**
- **Intelligence Networks**: Infiltrate OGW networks
- **Community Engagement**: Win hearts and minds
- **Legal Action**: Under UAPA
- **Rehabilitation**: For those willing to leave
- **Economic Opportunities**: Reduce unemployment
:::

:::details **Cyber Extremism**
**Concept:**
- Use of internet for extremist purposes
- Propaganda dissemination
- Recruitment
- Training material sharing
- Coordination of attacks

**Platforms Used:**
- **Social Media**: Facebook, Twitter, Instagram
- **Encrypted Apps**: Telegram, WhatsApp, Signal
- **Dark Web**: Anonymous forums
- **Gaming Platforms**: Recruiting youth

**Activities:**
- **Propaganda Videos**: Execution videos, ideological content
- **E-magazines**: Inspire, Dabiq (ISIS publications)
- **Training Manuals**: Bomb-making, weapons
- **Crowdfunding**: Bitcoin, cryptocurrency
- **Virtual Communities**: Echo chambers

**Challenges:**
- **Anonymity**: Difficult to trace
- **Encryption**: End-to-end encryption
- **Jurisdictional Issues**: Global platforms
- **Speed**: Rapid dissemination
- **Tech-Savvy Terrorists**: Using latest tools

**Counter-Measures:**
- **IT Rules 2021**: Intermediary guidelines
- **Content Removal**: Take-down notices
- **Counter-Narrative**: Alternative messaging
- **Cyber Cells**: In police departments
- **International Cooperation**: Cross-border coordination
- **AI and ML**: Automated detection of extremist content
:::

:::details **Combating Bioterrorism**
**Threat:**
- Deliberate release of biological agents
- Bacteria, viruses, toxins
- Mass casualties potential
- Panic and disruption

**Vulnerabilities:**
- Dense population
- Limited bio-security infrastructure
- Inadequate stockpiles of vaccines/antibiotics
- Weak disease surveillance

**Agents of Concern:**
- **Anthrax**: Bacterial spores
- **Smallpox**: Highly contagious virus
- **Plague**: Bacterial infection
- **Botulinum**: Deadly toxin
- **Ricin**: Plant-based toxin

**India's Preparedness:**
- **Biological Weapons Convention**: Signatory (1972)
- **National Disaster Management Authority**: Bio-disaster plans
- **Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP)**
- **National Centre for Disease Control (NCDC)**
- **Bio-Safety Level 4 Labs**: Limited number

**Gaps:**
- Inadequate laboratories
- Limited trained personnel
- Weak early warning systems
- Insufficient stockpiles
- Poor inter-agency coordination

**Way Forward:**
- **Strengthen Surveillance**: Early detection systems
- **Stockpiling**: Vaccines, antibiotics, PPE
- **Lab Infrastructure**: More BSL-3, BSL-4 labs
- **Training**: Healthcare workers, first responders
- **International Cooperation**: WHO, international conventions
- **Research**: Indigenous vaccine development
- **Drills and Exercises**: Regular preparedness drills
:::

## EVALUATION AND WAY FORWARD

:::details **Evaluation of Counter-Terrorism Response**
**Strengths:**
- **Strong Intelligence**: IB, RAW capabilities
- **Legal Framework**: Comprehensive laws (UAPA, NIA Act)
- **Political Will**: Zero tolerance policy
- **Military Capability**: Surgical strikes capability
- **International Cooperation**: FATF, UN resolutions

**Weaknesses:**
- **Inter-Agency Coordination**: Still gaps
- **Intelligence Failure**: Pulwama (2019), Pathankot (2016)
- **Police Modernization**: Slow progress
- **Conviction Rate**: Low in terror cases
- **Rehabilitation**: No comprehensive program
- **Radicalization Prevention**: Ad-hoc measures

**Second ARC Recommendations:**
- National Counter-Terrorism Centre (not implemented)
- Dedicated counter-terrorism force
- Comprehensive database (NATGRID ongoing)
- Witness protection program
- Faster trials in terror cases
- Regional cooperation framework
:::

:::details **Way Forward**
**Institutional Reforms:**
- **NCTC Implementation**: Federal structure concerns addressed
- **Witness Protection**: Enacted in 2018, effective implementation needed
- **NATGRID**: Complete integration of databases
- **Police Modernization**: SMART policing nationwide
- **Cyber Capabilities**: Dedicated cyber counter-terrorism units

**Legislative Measures:**
- **Sunset Clauses**: Periodic review of stringent laws
- **Safeguards**: Against misuse
- **Terror Financing**: Stronger laws
- **Cyberterrorism**: Specific provisions

**Operational Improvements:**
- **Border Security**: Complete fencing, technology integration
- **Coastal Security**: After 26/11 lessons
- **Aviation Security**: Updated protocols
- **Critical Infrastructure**: Protection enhanced
- **Soft Targets**: Security measures

**Prevention:**
- **De-radicalization**: National program
- **Counter-Narrative**: Credible voices
- **Education**: Critical thinking, pluralism
- **Economic Opportunities**: Reduce vulnerability
- **Community Policing**: Local intelligence

**International Cooperation:**
- **FATF**: Ensure Pakistan compliance
- **UN**: Comprehensive Convention on International Terrorism
- **Bilateral**: Enhanced cooperation with neighbors
- **Intelligence Sharing**: Real-time information exchange
- **Extradition Treaties**: Faster extradition processes
:::
