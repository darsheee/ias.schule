## BASICS OF SECURITY

:::details **Introduction and Meaning**
**Internal Security:**
- Act of preserving and maintaining the unity and integrity of the state
- Maintaining law and order within the territory
- Upholding sovereignty from disruptive and anti-national forces

**External Security:**
- Protecting country against external threats from foreign countries
- Dealing with terrorism, war, and foreign aggression

**Key Components:**
- Peace at borders and maintenance of law and order
- Collaboration between internal security forces and external forces
- Intelligence agencies coordination
- Economic growth and increased income levels
:::

:::details **Classification of Internal Security Threats**
**1. External Threats:**
- Cross-border terrorism (e.g., Jaish-e-Mohammad, LeT)
- War with neighboring countries
- Foreign intelligence activities
- Fourth Generation Warfare

**2. Internal Threats:**
- Separatist/Secessionist tendencies (Naxalism, regional movements)
- Communal activities and violence
- Caste conflicts
- Social unrest

**3. Internally Aided External Threats:**
- Support by extremist organizations to foreign nations
- Fifth column activities
- Espionage networks

**4. Externally Aided Internal Threats:**
- ISI funding and support
- Information warfare
- Electoral interference (e.g., Cambridge Analytica)
- Cyber attacks
:::

:::details **Factors Responsible for Internal Security Problems**
**Social Factors:**
- Communal divide and religious tensions
- Caste-based conflicts
- Poor human capital development
- Social media misuse and fake news
- Regional disparities

**Economic Factors:**
- Unemployment and demographic disaster
- Income inequality
- Poverty and lack of opportunities
- Regional economic disparity

**Political Factors:**
- Inter-state disputes (boundaries, water)
- Separatist tendencies
- Vote bank politics
- Administrative and governance failures
- Criminalization of politics (4,984 criminal charges against legislators as of Dec 2021)

**Ideological Factors:**
- Religious fundamentalism
- Maoism and left-wing extremism
- Pan-Islamic ideology

**External Factors:**
- Unfriendly neighbors (Pakistan's proxy war)
- Porous borders (Myanmar, Pakistan, Bangladesh)
- NGO interference
- Fourth Generation Warfare

**Environmental Factors:**
- Climate change-induced migration
- Disaster risks
- Resource scarcity
:::

:::details **Measures to Strengthen Internal Security**
**Legislative Measures:**
- Section 153A IPC: Prohibits creating disharmony between groups
- Section 123(3A) of RPA, 1951: Electoral provisions
- Section 69A of IT Act, 2000: Content blocking
- IT Rules 2021: Social media regulation

**Administrative Reforms:**
- **SMART Policing**: Strict & Sensitive, Modern & Mobile, Alert & Accountable, Reliable & Responsive, Techno-savvy & Trained
- Criminal justice system reform
- National Counter-Terrorism Centre
- Border management improvements

**Economic Measures:**
- Targeted poverty reduction
- Black money curbing (FEOA 2018, Benami Act 2016, PMLA 2002)
- Employment generation

**Military & Intelligence:**
- CAPF reforms
- Border infrastructure (BRO)
- Smart fencing and technology integration
- NATGRID for integrated intelligence
:::

:::details **Government Initiatives**
**Key Programs:**
- **UAPA Amendment Act, 2019**: Enhanced NIA powers
- **SAMADHAN Initiative**: Holistic LWE strategy
- **Modernization of Police**: ASMP scheme

## PRINCIPLES OF INDIA'S SECURITY POLICY

:::details **Nuclear Doctrine of India**
**Core Principles:**

1. **No First Use (NFU):**
   - Nuclear weapons only as retaliation
   - Retaliation against nuclear attack on Indian territory or forces

2. **Credible Minimum Deterrence:**
   - Minimum nuclear arsenal needed to deter adversaries
   - Second-strike capability

3. **Nuclear Deterrence of CBW:**
   - Option to use nuclear weapons against chemical/biological warfare

4. **Massive Retaliation:**
   - Unacceptable damage to adversary
   - Ensures deterrence

5. **Command Control:**
   - Political leadership controls nuclear deterrents
   - Nuclear Command Authority (Political + Executive Council)

6. **Not Against Non-Nuclear States:**
   - Commitment not to use against non-nuclear states

7. **Multilateral Disarmament:**
   - Support for global non-discriminatory disarmament

8. **Non-Proliferation:**
   - Strict export controls on nuclear and missile materials

**Advantages of NFU:**
- Reduces infrastructure costs
- Saves India from war initiation blame
- No chance of false alarm launches
- Builds international image
- Demonstrates responsible nuclear power

**Disadvantages:**
- Removes strategic ambiguity
- Allows continued aggression from adversaries
- Difficult to survive first attack
- Inefficiency of deterrence against conventional attacks
:::

## HYBRID WARFARE

:::details **Concept and Dimensions**
**Definition:**
- Combination of conventional and unconventional warfare tools
- Economic warfare, cyber attacks, lone wolf attacks, local unrest support

**Examples:**
- Russian cyber attacks
- Chinese "debt trap" diplomacy
- Information warfare
- Proxy wars

**Key Dimensions:**
- **Multiple Attack Vectors**: Economic, cyber, asymmetric
- **Difficult to Trace**: Non-state actors, cyber origins
- **Hard to Retaliate**: Ambiguity in attribution
- **Targets Vulnerabilities**: Social fault lines, cyber weaknesses
- **Democratic Undermining**: Coups, misinformation
- **Flexibility**: Multiple simultaneous fronts
- **Long-term Strategy**: Low visibility, fewer casualties
- **Psychological Warfare**: Social media, ideological attraction

**India's Response:**
- Wholesome approach across ministries
- Civilian-sensitive forces
- Regular vulnerability assessments
- Armed forces training in new technologies
- Cyber infrastructure building
- Strengthening democratic institutions
:::

- Leaders: Charu Majumdar, Kanu Sanyal, Jangal Santhal
- Communist Party of India (Marxist-Leninist) formed in 1969

**Current Status:**
- Red Corridor: Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand, Bihar, Andhra Pradesh
- Violence declined 77% (2258 incidents in 2009 â†’ 509 in 2021)
- Deaths declined 85% (1005 in 2010 â†’ 147 in 2021)
- 90% fatalities in Chhattisgarh

**Evolution Phases:**
1. **1967-75**: Peasant uprisings, CPI(ML) formation
2. **1975-2004**: People's War Group (1980), MCCI strengthening
3. **2004 onwards**: CPI(Maoist) merger, Red Corridor consolidation
:::

:::details **Factors Responsible for Naxalism**
**Land-Related:**
- Forest Conservation Act 1980 restrictions on tribals
- **Jal-Jangal-Jameen (3Js)** issues
- Land encroachment
- Non-regularization of traditional rights
- Inadequate compensation for acquisition

**Political:**
- Apathy towards tribal communities
- Lack of political participation
- Administrative failures

**Economic:**
- Poverty and unemployment
- Widening income gap
- Agrarian distress
- Lack of infrastructure (roads, electricity, healthcare, education)

**Environmental:**
- Mining and industrial activities
- Land and water resource destruction
- Displacement without rehabilitation
:::

:::details **Issues and Way Forward**

- Human rights concerns

**Way Forward:**

## INSURGENCY IN NORTH-EAST INDIA

:::details **Historical Background and Importance**
**Historical Context:**
- Integration post-independence
- Inner Line Permit system
- Article 371 special provisions
- Sixth Schedule autonomous councils

**Strategic Importance:**
- Gateway to South-East Asia
- Act East Policy corridor
- Rich biodiversity and natural resources
- Cultural diversity (200+ tribes)
- Border with China, Myanmar, Bangladesh, Bhutan

**Constitutional Provisions:**
- **Article 371A**: Nagaland special provisions
- **Article 371B**: Assam special provisions  
- **Article 371C**: Manipur special provisions
- **Article 371G**: Mizoram special provisions
- **Sixth Schedule**: Tribal autonomous councils
:::

:::details **Factors Responsible for Insurgency**
**Political:**
- Delayed statehood demands
- Perceived neglect by center
- Poor governance
- Corruption

**Economic:**
- Underdevelopment
- Lack of industrialization
- Poor connectivity
- Limited employment

**Social:**
- Ethnic conflicts
- Immigration issues (especially from Bangladesh)
- Cultural preservation concerns
- Tribal vs non-tribal tensions

**External:**
- China's support to insurgent groups
- Myanmar's porous border
- Pakistan's ISI involvement
- Bangladesh infiltration

**Historical:**
- Forced merger concerns
- British colonial policies
- Partition trauma
:::

:::details **State-Wise Insurgency Status**
**Assam:**
- **ULFA** (United Liberation Front of Assam)
- Immigration issues (Bangladeshi infiltration)
- Bodo insurgency (now resolved via Bodo Accord 2020)

**Nagaland:**
- Naga insurgency (oldest in India since 1950s)
- NSCN(IM) peace talks ongoing
- Framework Agreement 2015
- Separate flag and constitution demand

**Manipur:**
- Ethnic conflicts (Meitei, Naga, Kuki)
- UNLF, PLA, PREPAK groups
- Border disputes with Nagaland

**Tripura:**
- NLFT (National Liberation Front of Tripura)
- Insurgency largely controlled
- Immigration concerns

**Mizoram:**
- Largely peaceful post-1986 Mizo Accord
- Success story of conflict resolution

**Meghalaya:**
- HNLC (Hynniewtrep National Liberation Council)
- Low-intensity insurgency
- Border disputes with Assam
:::

:::details **Armed Forces Special Powers Act (AFSPA), 1958**
**Provisions:**
- Declare areas as "disturbed"
- Armed forces can arrest without warrant
- Search premises without warrant
- Shoot to kill (if necessary for maintaining order)
- Protection from prosecution (need GoI sanction)

**Arguments in Favor:**
- Necessary for counter-insurgency operations
- Protects armed forces from malicious prosecution
- Deters insurgents
- Facilitates quick action

**Arguments Against:**
- Human rights violations (fake encounters)
- Lack of accountability
- Irom Sharmila's 16-year fast
- Manorama case (2004)
- UN criticism
- Perpetuates cycle of violence

**Recent Developments:**
- Removed from Meghalaya (2018)
- Partially lifted in Arunachal Pradesh
- Reduced area in Nagaland, Assam
- Demands for complete repeal continue

**Way Forward:**
- Time-bound review of disturbed areas
- Accountability mechanisms
- Replace with humane law
- Focus on development over militarization
:::

## MILITANCY IN JAMMU & KASHMIR

:::details **Factors Responsible**
**Historical:**
- Accession controversy (1947)
- Article 370 provisions
- Plebiscite promise (unfulfilled)
- 1965, 1971, 1999 wars

**Political:**
- Rigged elections (1987 elections watershed)
- Political instability
- Governor's rule frequently
- Lack of good governance

**External:**
- Pakistan's proxy war
- ISI support to militants
- Training camps in PoK
- China's tacit support

**Social:**
- Radicalization of youth
- Hawkish narrative
- Pellet gun injuries
- Civilian casualties

**Economic:**
- Underdevelopment in certain areas
- Unemployment
- Tourism disruption
- Investment deterrence
:::

:::details **Kashmir: Battleground of Proxy War**
**Pakistan's Strategy:**
- **1990s**: Peak of militancy (Kashmiri Pandits exodus)
- **Infiltration**: Through LoC, tunnels
- **Terror Groups**: LeT, JeM, HM, Al-Badr
- **Stone Pelting**: Using youth as shield
- **Narrative Building**: International forums

**Major Attacks:**
- 2001 Parliament Attack
- 2008 Mumbai Attacks
- 2016 Uri Attack
- 2019 Pulwama Attack

**India's Response:**
- Surgical Strikes (2016)
- Balakot Air Strike (2019)
- Article 370 Abrogation (2019)
- Counter-infiltration grid
- Zero tolerance for terror
:::

:::details **Removal of Article 370 and Impact**
**Article 370 Abrogation (August 5, 2019):**
- J&K bifurcated into 2 UTs (J&K and Ladakh)
- Special status removed
- Indian laws fully applicable
- Article 35A also nullified

**Expected Impact on Militancy:**
- **Positive:**
  - Integration with national mainstream
  - Economic development boost
  - Land reforms
  - Investment opportunities
  - Rule of law strengthening

- **Challenges:**
  - Initial lockdown and communication blockade
  - Political leaders' detention
  - International criticism (especially Pakistan)
  - Local resentment
  - Need for political process restoration

**Recent Trends:**
- Targeted killings of minorities, migrants
- Hybrid terrorists emerging
- Drone attacks increasing
- But overall violence decreasing
:::

:::details **Strategies to Counter Militancy**
**Security Measures:**
- Enhanced surveillance
- Intelligence-based operations
- Counter-infiltration grid
- Technology use (drones, sensors)
- Border fencing and lighting

**Government Initiatives:**
- **PMDP**: Prime Minister's Development Package
- **Back to Village Programme**: Grassroots engagement
- **UDBP**: Urban Development projects
- **Rehabilitation**: Surrender policy
- **De-radicalization**: Countering extremist ideology

**Civic Engagement:**
- Sports activities for youth
- Skill development programs
- Employment generation
- Cultural programs
- Educational opportunities

**Way Forward:**
- Restore political process
- Grassroots democracy (Panchayat elections held)
- Economic development
- End cross-border terrorism
- Dialogue with all stakeholders
- Address genuine grievances
:::
